# Appliance Maintenance Basics

## Overview
General maintenance principles apply across all appliances. Consistent care extends lifespan 30-50%, improves efficiency, prevents failures, and reduces repair costs significantly.

## Universal Maintenance Principles

**Cleanliness Is Critical**: Dust, debris, and buildup reduce efficiency and cause overheating.
- Clean filters/vents monthly
- Wipe exterior weekly
- Remove internal debris annually
- Prevent dirt accumulation inside

**Keep Appliances Dry**: Moisture damages electrical components.
- Wipe spills immediately
- Don't leave wet
- Store in dry location
- Ventilation prevents moisture accumulation

**Proper Storage Prevents Damage**:
- Keep away from heat sources
- Avoid extreme temperature swings
- Protect from direct sunlight
- Ensure adequate ventilation space

## Preventive Maintenance Schedule

**Daily (5 minutes)**:
- Visual inspection for abnormalities
- Check for unusual smells/sounds
- Feel exterior for excessive heat

**Weekly (15 minutes)**:
- Clean exterior surfaces
- Remove visible dust/debris
- Check for leaks or moisture
- Test basic functionality

**Monthly (30 minutes)**:
- Deep clean accessible areas
- Inspect power cord
- Run efficiency check (performance test)
- Check for wear signs

**Quarterly (1 hour)**:
- Clean filters/vents thoroughly
- Inspect for rust/corrosion spots
- Check all connections (tight/secure)
- Document any changes

**Annually (2 hours)**:
- Professional inspection
- Complete internal cleaning
- Replace wear items (seals, filters)
- System-wide testing

**Every 2-5 Years**:
- Major component replacement (planned obsolescence)
- Full system overhaul
- Upgrade consideration if efficiency declining

## Component-Specific Maintenance

**Filters**:
- Air filters: Clean monthly, replace quarterly
- Water filters: Replace per manufacturer (typically 3-6 months)
- Drain filters: Clean monthly (prevents clogs)

**Seals/Gaskets**:
- Inspect for cracks/deterioration quarterly
- Replace if damage visible (prevents leaks)
- Typical lifespan: 5-7 years

**Electrical Connections**:
- Tighten loose connections annually
- Check for corrosion (green/white buildup)
- Replace burnt/damaged connectors

**Motors/Pumps**:
- Listen for unusual sounds monthly
- Feel for excessive heat
- Verify smooth operation
- Replace bearings if grinding noise

**Control Boards**:
- Protect from moisture
- Ensure proper ventilation
- Avoid extreme temperature exposure
- Professional service if malfunction suspected

## Signs Your Appliance Needs Maintenance

**Performance Decrease**:
- Reduced output power
- Slower operation
- Takes longer to complete cycles
- Less efficient results

**Unusual Sounds**:
- Grinding, squealing, buzzing
- Louder than baseline
- Intermittent vs constant noise

**Temperature Issues**:
- Exterior hotter than normal
- Interior not reaching target temperature
- Hot spots in unexpected areas

**Electrical Signs**:
- Breaker tripping frequently
- Cord warm to touch
- Burnt smell
- Sparking/arcing visible

**Leaks/Moisture**:
- Water pooling
- Condensation excessive
- Wet connections
- Corrosion visible

## DIY vs Professional Maintenance

**DIY Maintenance Safe For**:
- Cleaning (interior, exterior, filters)
- Visual inspections
- Tightening loose bolts/connections
- Power cord checking
- Filter replacement

**Requires Professional Service**:
- Electrical circuit repair
- Component replacement (motor, compressor, heating element)
- Refrigerant handling (AC, refrigerator)
- High-voltage capacitor work
- Any internal electronic component

**When In Doubt**: Call professional. Mistakes on electrical appliances dangerous.

## Maintenance Logs

**Keep Simple Records**:
- Date of maintenance
- Work performed
- Parts replaced
- Cost
- Technician details
- Next recommended service

**Tracking Benefits**:
- Identifies recurring issues
- Predicts failure timing
- Justifies replacement decisions
- Tracks warranty coverage

## Seasonal Maintenance Adjustments

**Summer**:
- Increase cleaning frequency (dust/pollen)
- Monitor cooling (AC, coolers) extra carefully
- Ensure ventilation unobstructed
- Inspect for corrosion (heat stress)

**Monsoon**:
- Increase drying frequency
- Check for moisture/leaks
- Verify outlets dry
- Protect from water ingress

**Winter**:
- Monitor electrical consumption (higher loads)
- Check heating appliances thoroughly
- Ensure thermostats calibrated
- Verify connections secure
