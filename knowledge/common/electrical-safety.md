# Electrical Safety for Appliances

## Overview
Critical electrical safety principles for all home appliances. Improper use causes electrocution, electrical fires, and equipment damage. Understanding and practicing electrical safety protects lives and property.

## Understanding Electricity Basics

**Voltage (230V)": Standard household voltage in India. Sufficient to cause electrocution (lethal threshold is 50V AC).

**Current (Amperes)**: Flow of electricity. 1 amp = lethal. Household breakers typically 16-32 amps.

**Resistance": Opposition to current flow. Water conducts electricity. Dry skin ~100,000 ohms resistance. Wet skin ~1,000 ohms (much more dangerous).

**Power (Watts)": Energy consumed. Heating element = high power (2000-4000W). Motor = 500-1500W.

## Electrical Hazards Overview

**Electrocution": Electric current passing through body. 1-5 mA = shock sensation. 5-20 mA = muscle contractions. >100 mA = cardiac arrest/death. 230V household current easily causes death.

**Electrical Burns": Current heating skin/tissue. Burns can be severe internally even if skin looks minor.

**Electrical Fire": Arcing (jumping electricity) ignites nearby materials. Often unnoticed until fire spreads.

**Electrical Shock": Non-lethal but painful. Can cause falls or sudden reactions causing injury.

## Safe Electrical Practices

**Never Use In Wet Conditions": Water conducts electricity perfectly. Never use appliances:
- With wet hands
- Near bathtubs/sinks when wet
- On wet floors
- During rain near windows
- With damp outlets

**Use Proper Outlets": 
- Dedicated outlet for high-power appliances (not shared)
- Proper grounding (three-pin outlet with earth connection)
- Never use damaged outlets
- Replace cracked outlet covers immediately

**Use Voltage Stabilizer": 
- Minimum 2 kVA for large appliances
- Protects from voltage surges
- Protects internal electronic components
- Prevents component burnout

**Check Power Cords Regularly":
- Inspect monthly for damage
- Look for fraying, cuts, discoloration
- Never use damaged cords
- Replace cord if insulation compromised
- Keep cords away from heat sources

**Avoid Extension Cords For Large Appliances":
- Extension cords not rated for high current
- Fire hazard with heavy loads
- Use dedicated outlet instead
- If extension necessary, heavy-gauge wire (1.5mm²) only

**Never Repair Appliances While Powered": 
- Unplug before any service
- Wait 5 minutes after unplugging (capacitor discharge)
- Verify unplugged before touching internal components

**Use Circuit Breakers/Fuses":
- Breaker should trip if excessive current detected
- Normal breakers: 16A for appliances, 32A for water heater
- Tripped breaker = warning sign, don't ignore
- Never bypass breaker or use incorrect amperage

## Electrical Safety for Specific Appliances

**Microwave": 
- Never operate with door open (radiation hazard, not electrical, but safety concern)
- Never repair high voltage circuits (capacitor dangerous)
- Keep moisture away from vent areas

**Washing Machine**:
- Ensure machine on level surface (prevents vibration creating electrical hazard)
- Don't pull on power cord (stresses wiring)
- Use dedicated outlet (high current draw)

**Geyser**:
- Use 16A minimum breaker
- Install voltage stabilizer
- Dedicated outlet critical (constant high load)
- Never touch heating element while powered

**Refrigerator**:
- Ensure outlet works properly (refrigerator runs continuously)
- Use ice or dry cloth to clean condenser, not water (avoid electrical contact)

**AC/Cooler**:
- High power consumption (need 32A breaker typically)
- Dedicated outlet strongly recommended
- Never touch compressor or motor while running

## Emergency Electrical Procedures

**If Someone Is Electrocuted**:
1. Don't touch person (you'll be electrocuted)
2. Turn off power immediately (breaker/unplug appliance)
3. If power cannot be accessed, use non-conductive object (wooden stick) to separate person from source
4. Call ambulance immediately
5. Perform CPR if person unconscious and not breathing

**If Electrical Fire Starts**:
1. Don't use water (water conducts electricity, spreads fire)
2. Use dry chemical fire extinguisher (Class C) or baking soda
3. Unplug appliance if safe to do so (don't approach if fire spreading)
4. Call fire service immediately
5. Evacuate if fire spreads

**If You Feel Electrical Shock**:
1. Unplug or turn off breaker immediately
2. Don't touch appliance
3. Inspect for damage
4. Do not use until professionally checked

## Appliance-Specific Electrical Tips

**Before Plugging In Any Appliance**:
- Check cord for damage
- Verify outlet is not damaged
- Confirm outlet is grounded (three holes)
- Verify breaker capacity adequate

**Grounding Verification**:
- Three-pin outlet (earth pin larger/round)
- Single-phase outlet has one earth pin
- Always use three-pin earthed outlet if available
- Never force three-pin into two-pin outlet

**Regular Electrical Inspections**:
- Electrician should inspect home wiring annually
- Check for loose connections, burnt wires, arcing signs
- Verify breaker labeling matches loads
- Test ELCB (Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker) monthly

## Voltage Stabilizer Selection

**Power Requirement Calculator**:
- Add wattage of all simultaneous appliances
- Add 20% safety margin
- Choose stabilizer capacity accordingly
- Example: 2000W geyser + 1200W AC = 3200W total → 4 kVA stabilizer minimum

**Stabilizer Types**:
- Servo stabilizer: Best protection, more expensive
- Relay stabilizer: Good protection, moderate cost
- Automatic: Good for most homes
- Manual: Requires adjustment (not recommended for 24/7 appliances)

## Electrical Safety Maintenance Schedule

**Daily**: Visual inspection for unusual smells, heat, sounds
**Weekly**: Check outlets for heat/damage
**Monthly**: Inspect power cords, test ELCB
**Quarterly**: Verify breaker operation (test trip)
**Annually**: Professional electrical inspection, ELCB testing
**After Power Outage**: Check all appliances before use (surge damage possible)

## Cost of Electrical Safety Investment

- Voltage stabilizer (2 kVA): ₹2000-4000
- ELCB installation: ₹1500-3000
- Proper wiring upgrade: ₹5000-15000
- Professional inspection: ₹1000-2000

**Prevention Saves**: Thousands in fire damage, equipment replacement, medical costs

## Signs of Electrical Problems Requiring Professional Help

- Breaker constantly tripping
- Outlets warm to touch
- Burning smell from outlet/cord
- Flickering lights (when appliance runs)
- Visible arcing or sparking
- Buzzing sounds from outlets
- Discolored/melted outlet cover
