# Emergency Shutdown Procedures

## Overview
Life-saving procedures for dangerous appliance situations. Quick, correct action prevents injury, death, and property damage. Every household member should know basic emergency shutdowns.

## Universal Emergency Shutdown Procedure

**For Any Life-Threatening Situation**:
1. **Don't panic** - calm action prevents mistakes
2. **Identify immediate danger** - electrical, fire, gas, water/flood, mechanical
3. **Get yourself to safety first** - cannot help if you're injured
4. **Call for help if needed** - ambulance (102) or fire (101)
5. **Address danger only if safe** - don't risk yourself for appliance

**General Shutdown Sequence**:
1. Turn off appliance power switch (if accessible/safe)
2. Unplug appliance from outlet (if safe to approach)
3. Turn off breaker for that circuit
4. Close water inlet valve (if water-related emergency)
5. Leave appliance alone until professional assessment

## Specific Emergency Situations

### ELECTRICAL EMERGENCIES

**If Someone Is Electrocuted**:
1. **Do not touch the person** - you will be electrocuted
2. **Turn off power immediately**:
   - Flip breaker OFF
   - Unplug appliance (if cord visible and reachable)
   - Use non-conductive object (wooden stick, rubber handle) to separate person from source if power inaccessible
3. **Call ambulance immediately** - 102
4. **If person unconscious**:
   - Check breathing
   - Position recovery position (on side)
   - Start CPR if trained and breathing absent (30 chest compressions, 2 rescue breaths, repeat)
5. **Keep scene clear** - let medical professionals work

**If You Feel Electrical Shock**:
1. **Stop touching appliance** - break contact immediately
2. **Turn off power** - flip breaker or unplug
3. **Sit down** - wait for trembling to stop
4. **Assess yourself** - any burns or pain?
5. **Do not use appliance again** - professional inspection required

**If Electrical Sparking/Arcing Visible**:
1. **Turn off power immediately** - unplug or flip breaker
2. **Do not touch appliance** - wait 5 minutes after unplugging
3. **Allow to cool** - wait 30 minutes if very hot
4. **Do not reuse** - professional inspection mandatory

**If Cord Is Burning/Smoking**:
1. **Turn off breaker immediately** (don't unplug if cord burning—fire risk)
2. **Use fire extinguisher** (Class C, electrical) if fire small
3. **Evacuate immediately** if fire spreading
4. **Call fire service** - 101

### FIRE EMERGENCIES

**If Fire Starts Inside Appliance**:
1. **Do not open door/cover** (stops oxygen flow, fire suffocates)
2. **Turn off power** - unplug or flip breaker
3. **Do not use water** - water conducts electricity, may spread fire
4. **Use Class C fire extinguisher** (electrical fires) if trained:
   - Aim at base of fire
   - Sweep side to side
   - Maintain safe distance
5. **If fire spreading or won't stop**:
   - Evacuate immediately
   - Call fire service - 101
   - Alert neighbors

**If Fire Spreads to Room**:
1. **Evacuate immediately** - appliance not worth life
2. **Alert everyone in house** - wake sleeping family
3. **Use staircase** (not elevator)
4. **Close doors behind you** - slows fire spread
5. **Call fire service** - 101 from outside
6. **Gather in safe spot** outside - away from building

**If Trapped by Fire**:
1. **Close door** - prevent fire from entering
2. **Stuff towel under door** - block smoke
3. **Go to window** - wave cloth to signal help
4. **Stay low** - heat and smoke rise
5. **Call for help** - phone 101 if possible
6. **Don't jump** unless safe (firefighters have rescue equipment)

### WATER EMERGENCIES

**If Appliance Leaking Heavily**:
1. **Turn off appliance** - stop the leak source
2. **Turn off water inlet valve** (usually behind/under appliance):
   - Rotate valve fully clockwise
   - Stops water flow immediately
3. **Unplug appliance** - electrical hazard with water
4. **Place towels** - contain water spread
5. **Use mop/wet vac** - remove standing water
6. **Ventilate area** - open windows, increase airflow
7. **Check for electrical contact** - water near outlet/breaker?

**If Water Leaking Toward Outlet/Electrical**:
1. **Turn off breaker immediately** - electrical hazard
2. **Turn off water valve** - stop water source
3. **Do not touch outlet/wet area** - electrocution hazard
4. **Remove water** carefully from distance with mop/towels
5. **Allow to dry completely** (24+ hours) before restoring power
6. **Have electrician inspect** - confirm safety before restoration

**If Geyser Tank Leaking Heavily**:
1. **Turn off geyser immediately** - power off, unplug
2. **Turn off inlet water valve** - usually near geyser
3. **Open drain valve** (if present) - let water out into bucket
4. **Turn off main water supply** if leak doesn't stop
5. **Call plumber** - geyser may need replacement
6. **Do not attempt repair** - under pressure, dangerous

**If Washing Machine Flooding**:
1. **Stop cycle** - press emergency stop
2. **Open door** if it will unlock (wait if locked—in safety mode)
3. **Place towels** - block water from spreading
4. **Turn off inlet valves** (red and blue hoses behind machine):
   - Turn both fully clockwise
   - Stops water immediately
5. **Use plunger or wet vac** - remove water from drum
6. **Empty bucket** under drain valve (if accessible)

### GAS EMERGENCIES (If Applicable)

**If Smell Gas Near Appliance**:
1. **Do not switch anything on or off** - spark ignites gas
2. **Do not use lighter/matches** - ignition hazard
3. **Evacuate immediately** - leave house
4. **Call gas company emergency** - usually number on bill
5. **Don't re-enter** until cleared by professionals

### THERMAL EMERGENCIES

**If Appliance Exterior Dangerously Hot**:
1. **Turn off power** - unplug immediately
2. **Do not touch** - severe burn risk
3. **Keep distance** - hot appliance may ignite nearby materials
4. **Allow to cool** - wait 30+ minutes
5. **Provide ventilation** - improves cooling
6. **Professional inspection** - before reuse

**If Appliance Causing Extreme Temperature Rise in Room**:
1. **Turn off appliance** - stop heat source
2. **Increase ventilation** - open windows/doors
3. **Allow room to cool** - if temperature excessive
4. **Professional inspection** - check for malfunction

### MECHANICAL EMERGENCIES

**If Clothing/Hair Caught in Machine**:
1. **Turn off immediately** - stop machine
2. **Do not pull hard** - may tangle more
3. **Unplug appliance** - power off to prevent restart
4. **Gently untangle** - slow movements
5. **If stuck tight**:
   - Call emergency services - 102
   - Wait for professional help
   - Don't force extraction (causes injury)

**If Child's Hand/Arm Caught in Appliance**:
1. **Do not panic** - panic causes mistakes
2. **Turn off appliance** immediately
3. **Unplug appliance** - prevent unexpected restart
4. **Gently guide limb out** - reverse direction it went in
5. **If truly stuck**:
   - Call ambulance - 102
   - Don't pull forcefully
   - Await professional extraction
6. **Assess for injury** - check for cuts/circulation
7. **Seek medical attention** - even if appears minor (internal damage possible)

## Prevention Is Best Emergency Response

**Reduce Emergency Risk**:
- Regular maintenance (catch problems early)
- Keep fire extinguisher accessible (Class C for electrical)
- Know your home's electrical panel (practice breaker identification)
- Know where all water shutoff valves are
- Don't ignore warning signs
- Replace worn components before failure
- Use voltage stabilizers for surge protection
- Never DIY electrical repairs you're unsure about

## After Emergency: Recovery Steps

**Document for Insurance**:
- Take photos of damage
- Keep all receipts for repairs
- Document when issue started
- Note any injuries
- Report to insurance company

**Professional Assessment**:
- Have technician inspect appliance
- Get written diagnosis
- Verify appliance safe before reuse
- Consider replacement if extensive damage

**Prevent Recurrence**:
- Address underlying cause
- Implement preventive maintenance
- Upgrade safety equipment (better breakers, stabilizers)
- Consider replacement with safer models

## Summary: When to Call Emergency Services vs Technician

**Call 102 (Ambulance)**:
- Any person injured/hurt
- Electrocution/electric shock
- Severe burns
- Breathing difficulty

**Call 101 (Fire)**:
- Visible fire inside/outside appliance
- Fire spreading
- Smoke throughout building
- Cannot safely evacuate

**Call Technician Later**:
- Appliance malfunctioning but no immediate danger
- Minor leak
- Unusual sounds/smell (but appliance turned off)
- Performance degradation

**Call Both**:
- Major fire/injury occurring
- Always prioritize human safety over appliance
