# Washing Machine Drum Not Spinning

## Overview
Wash cycle runs but drum doesn't rotate. Water fills, detergent releases, but no tumbling motion. Clothes don't get washed. Caused by broken drive belt, pulley misalignment, clutch failure, or motor problems. Clothes may get soaked but not cleaned.

## Symptoms
- Water fills normally
- Cycle timer shows progress
- Motor hums but no drum rotation
- Clothes wet but not washed
- Squealing or grinding noise from drum area
- Drum feels loose when pushed (drum wobbles)
- Burning smell from belt area
- Error code E3 or similar

## Possible Causes
**Broken Drive Belt**: Belt connects motor to drum pulley. If broken, motor spins but drum doesn't rotate. Most common cause.

**Worn Drum Pulley**: Pulley grooves worn smooth. Belt slips instead of driving drum. Eventually belt breaks.

**Clutch Failure**: Friction device that engages drum to transmission. If worn, drum doesn't couple to motor.

**Motor Not Running**: Motor hums but rotor doesn't spin. Capacitor failure or rotor jam.

**Transmission Failure**: Complex gear system that transfers motor power to drum. Internal wear prevents drum rotation.

**Coupling Broken**: Rubber coupling between motor and transmission torn or disintegrated.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check for grinding noise**: When cycle runs, listen to drum area carefully. Grinding = worn pulley or clutch. Silence = belt broken.

2. **Spin drum manually**: Stop machine. Open drum. Can you spin drum by hand? Easy spinning = belt broken. Cannot spin = jam or mechanical lock.

3. **Check for visible belt**: Locate belt (usually behind drum front, accessible after removing front panel). Is belt present and intact? Any cracks or fraying?

4. **Inspect pulley**: Look at drum pulley (back of drum). Is surface smooth or grooved? Smooth = worn out.

5. **Check for burnt smell**: Belt area should have no burning smell. Burnt smell = excessive friction from worn pulley.

6. **Listen to motor**: Does motor sound normal when humming? Any high-pitched whine = motor bearing wear.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Main motor operates at 230V. Do not touch motor components while powered.

**Mechanical**: Rotating drum or motor is entanglement hazard. Always unplug before accessing internals.

**Hot Water**: Water in drum may be hot. Drain before opening or wear gloves.

## When To Call A Technician
- Belt visibly broken or severely worn
- Pulley surface smooth/worn (slipping)
- Grinding noise indicating clutch/transmission wear
- Motor not running despite humming
- Cannot manually spin drum (jam or lock)
- Burning smell indicating serious friction

## Preventive Maintenance
- Replace belt every 4-5 years (preventive before breaking)
- Avoid overloading machine (excess load strains belt)
- Use appropriate detergent (excess suds increase load)
- Don't interrupt cycle suddenly (wear on clutch)
- Allow motor to cool between cycles (rest 10 min between hot washes)
- Have annual inspection of belt condition
