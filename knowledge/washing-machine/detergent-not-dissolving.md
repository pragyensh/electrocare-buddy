# Washing Machine Detergent Not Dissolving

## Overview
Detergent remains in drawer or collects in drum after cycle. Clothes may have detergent residue. Detergent effectiveness reduced. Caused by low water temperature, insufficient water pressure, water not reaching drawer, or clogged dispenser.

## Symptoms
- Powder detergent visible in drawer after cycle
- Detergent clumps in drum
- White powder residue on clothes
- Poor cleaning despite adequate detergent
- Detergent drawer not emptying
- Water pressure weak from dispenser
- Sweet detergent smell on clothes

## Possible Causes
**Low Water Temperature**: Cold water doesn't dissolve powder detergent efficiently. Needs warm water.

**Insufficient Water Flow": Water pressure too low to flush detergent through system.

**Clogged Dispenser": Detergent residue buildup blocks dispenser nozzle or channel.

**Drawer Not Aligned": Drawer not clicking into place. Water doesn't flow through it.

**Water Inlet Valve Issue": Valve doesn't open fully. Reduced water flow to dispenser.

**Cycle Selection": Used cold wash cycle when detergent requires warm water.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check water temperature setting": Review cycle selected. Is it cold water, warm water, or hot water cycle? Cold cycles may not dissolve powder detergent effectively. Switch to warm cycle.

2. **Inspect dispenser drawer": Open drawer fully. Is it clean or have detergent residue buildup? Soak drawer in hot water for 30 minutes. Scrub gently to remove residue. Dry and reinstall.

3. **Clean dispenser channels": Look for channels/slots in main body where drawer water flows from. Any visible detergent buildup? Use small brush or cloth to clean out.

4. **Check drawer position": Close drawer. Does it click firmly into place? Or does it feel loose? Loose drawer = water doesn't flow through it. Try repositioning and closing firmly.

5. **Test water pressure": Run fill cycle. Look at water spraying into drum. Is pressure strong or weak? Weak pressure = reduced water reaching dispenser.

6. **Use correct detergent type": Some machines require liquid detergent (powder clogs more easily). Check manual. Consider switching to liquid if powder problematic.

7. **Adjust detergent amount": Excessive detergent undissolved. Use less (recommended amount on detergent bottle). More detergent ≠ cleaner clothes. Proper amount works better.

8. **Select appropriate cycle": Shorter cycles with less water dissolve detergent harder. Use normal/full cycle with adequate water. Check manual for detergent recommendations per cycle.

## Safety Warnings
**Detergent Residue**: Undissolved detergent may irritate skin. Rinse clothes extra if residue visible.

**Machine Damage": Detergent buildup can clog internal passages over time.

## When To Call A Technician
- Dispenser channels severely clogged (require professional cleaning)
- Water pressure issue (inlet valve problem)
- Drawer mechanism broken (won't seat properly)

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Dispenser cleaning (professional): ₹600–₹1200
- Inlet water valve replacement: ₹1200–₹2500
- Dispenser drawer replacement: ₹800–₹1500

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean dispenser drawer monthly
- Use detergent amount recommended on bottle (not guesswork)
- Use warm water cycle for powder detergent
- Use liquid detergent in cold-water cycles
- Wipe dispenser area dry after each use
- Run empty hot cycle monthly with vinegar (clears residue)
