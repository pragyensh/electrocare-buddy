# Washing Machine Not Filling Water

## Overview
Wash cycle starts but water doesn't enter drum. Clothes remain dry. Caused by inlet valve failure, kinked inlet hose, clogged filter, low water pressure, or faulty fill sensor. Water must fill drum before washing.

## Symptoms
- Cycle runs but drum stays empty
- Very low water level (partial fill)
- Clothes dry after cycle
- Inlet valve making clicking sound (trying to open)
- Water pressure very low from inlet
- Error code E1 or fill-related code
- Fill valve making constant noise

## Possible Causes
**Inlet Valve Stuck/Faulty**: Solenoid valve that opens to allow water doesn't engage or gets stuck.

**Kinked or Blocked Inlet Hose**: Hose bent shut or debris inside blocks water flow.

**Clogged Water Filter**: Small screen filter at inlet gets plugged with sediment/mineral deposits.

**Low Water Supply Pressure**: Main water supply pressure too low (below 0.5 bar).

**Inlet Hose Not Connected**: Hose disconnected from machine or water source.

**Faulty Fill Sensor**: Sensor doesn't detect water presence. Machine stops fill prematurely.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check inlet hoses**: At back of machine, two hoses—cold (blue) and hot (red). Are they connected to taps? Turn on both taps and hold hose end over bucket. Water flows? If no water, house supply issue (not machine).

2. **Inspect hoses for kinks**: Trace hoses from taps to machine. Any bends or kinks? Straighten carefully.

3. **Check inlet hose filter**: At machine inlet (where hose connects), small mesh filter. Turn off water. Disconnect hose. Can you see mesh screen? Is it clogged with sediment/mineral deposits? Use magnifying glass. If very dirty, needs cleaning.

4. **Clean filter screen**: Use small brush or soak in vinegar (30 min) to dissolve mineral deposits. Rinse thoroughly. Reconnect.

5. **Test water pressure**: Turn on tap to maximum. Does water flow strongly? Weak flow = low pressure (wait for pressure boost or check building water supply).

6. **Listen to inlet valve**: When cycle starts fill, should hear click-click sound (solenoid engaging). No sound = solenoid dead. Constant clicking = solenoid failing.

7. **Check manual fill**: If machine has manual fill option, try filling by pouring water directly into drum. If drum fills, inlet valve definitely faulty.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Inlet solenoid operates at 230V AC. Do not touch while powered.

**Water Damage**: Blocked inlet creating pressure buildup may cause hose rupture. If you hear high-pitched hissing, turn off water immediately.

## When To Call A Technician
- Inlet valve not clicking/responding
- Water pressure tested and confirmed low (building issue requires plumber)
- Inlet hose has internal blockage
- Fill sensor faulty (won't respond to manual fill)
- Solenoid coil burned out (burnt smell near valve)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean inlet filter screen every 3-6 months
- Inspect inlet hoses annually for cracks/leaks
- Ensure hoses not kinked during storage
- Check tap screen filters (building water supply) quarterly
- Use water softener if hard water area (prevents mineral buildup)
- Turn off inlet taps when not using machine for extended periods
