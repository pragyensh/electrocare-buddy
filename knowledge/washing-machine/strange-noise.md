# Washing Machine Strange Noise

## Overview
Unusual noises coming from machine during operation. Squealing, grinding, buzzing, or rattling. Indicates mechanical wear, foreign objects, or electrical component issues. Noise level increases over time if issue not addressed.

## Symptoms
- High-pitched squealing from drum
- Grinding noise during rotation
- Buzzing sound from motor
- Rattling/clicking from side panels
- Metallic banging noise
- Loud grinding that sounds like rocks
- Squeaking from door area
- Noise only during spin cycle

## Possible Causes
**Worn Drum Bearing**: Bearing degrades, produces grinding or squealing sound.

**Drum Pulley Worn**: Pulley grooves worn smooth. Belt slips and squeals.

**Belt Fraying**: Worn belt makes grinding or squealing noise as it slips.

**Foreign Object**: Coin, bone, metal piece stuck between drum and tub. Creates rattling or metallic banging.

**Pump Cavitation**: Air in pump produces buzzing or grinding.

**Motor Bearing Wear**: Motor shaft bearing worn. Produces high-pitched whine or grinding.

**Faulty Door Lock": Solenoid clicking repeatedly. Normal, but excessive clicking indicates wear.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Identify noise type": Note exact sound character—squealing, grinding, rattling, or buzzing. Different sounds indicate different issues.

2. **Identify when noise occurs": Does it happen during fill, wash, spin, or drain? Specific timing narrows diagnosis.

3. **Check for foreign objects**: Open drum and inspect area between drum and outer tub wall. Any visible coins, wire, or debris? Shine flashlight around drum circumference.

4. **Reduce load and retry": Remove clothes. Run spin-only cycle with empty drum. Same noise = mechanical issue. Noise gone = load-related.

5. **Listen to motor**: When machine running, hold ear near motor area (side or back). Grinding/whining = motor bearing wear. Normal hum = okay.

6. **Feel drum smoothness": Manual drum rotation should be smooth. Any grittiness or rough spots = bearing wear.

7. **Check door lock": Open door. Close door. Listen for solenoid click. Single click = normal. Repeated clicking (every second) = solenoid wear.

## Safety Warnings
**Rotating Parts**: Never insert objects into moving drum to investigate noise. Wait for drum to stop.

**Electrical": Do not reach near motor or pulleys while powered. Entanglement hazard.

**Sudden Failure**: Severely worn bearing may seize suddenly, locking drum. Stop operation if noise worsens rapidly.

## When To Call A Technician
- Grinding noise from drum area (bearing wear)
- Squealing during spin (pulley/belt issue)
- Motor bearing whining sound
- Foreign object cannot be removed (stuck inside)
- Noise accompanied by reduced performance
- Noise level increasing rapidly over weeks

## Preventive Maintenance
- Empty all pockets before washing
- Run filter clean cycle monthly (removes debris)
- Don't wash metal objects (buttons OK if intact)
- Avoid washing items with hard materials (bones, shells)
- Monitor noise level—any increase indicates need for service
- Have bearing inspection annually on machines >5 years old
