# Washing Machine Leaking Water

## Overview
Water leaking from machine base, joints, or door. May occur during fill, wash, or drain cycles. Risk of electrical hazard, water damage to floors, and structural damage. Caused by cracked hose, loose connection, faulty door seal, or pump failure.

## Symptoms
- Water pooling under machine
- Water dripping during fill or wash
- Water leaking from door area
- Water coming from drain hose connection
- Wet floor surrounding machine
- Musty smell near machine (water retention)
- Reduced water level in drum
- Error code E5 or leak-related code

## Possible Causes
**Cracked Inlet Hose**: Visible crack in supply hose. Water leaks during fill or normal operation.

**Loose Hose Connection**: Hose not fully connected to machine or tap. Vibration causes disconnection.

**Faulty Door Seal**: Rubber gasket around door worn or torn. Water leaks from door.

**Cracked Drum**: Crack inside washing drum allows water to leak into tub during spin cycle.

**Faulty Pump**: Pump housing cracked or seal failed. Water leaks from bottom.

**Loose Internal Connections**: Vibration loosens internal pipe connections.

**Overflow**: Water level sensor failed. Too much water overflows from drum.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Identify leak source**: Run fill cycle. Observe carefully where water appears. Is it dripping from top connections, side, bottom, or door?

2. **Check inlet hoses**: Trace hoses from taps to machine inlet. Any visible cracks or moisture? Wet spots on hose indicate leak.

3. **Inspect connections**: At machine inlet, connections should be tight. Hand-tighten hose connection if you can access. Do not over-tighten (may crack).

4. **Check door seal**: Open machine. Inspect rubber gasket around drum door edge. Any tears, cracks, or flat spots where seal doesn't compress?

5. **Test door closure**: Close door firmly. Pour water directly into drum from above door. Watch for leaks around door frame. Water dripping from door = seal failure.

6. **Inspect drain hose connection**: Where drain hose connects to machine, should be clipped/screwed tightly. Try gently tightening connection (not while machine running).

7. **Look for standing water**: If leak occurs during spin, water accumulates around drum. Check for water inside tub (outside drum, in tub pocket).

8. **Check drain pump": At bottom of machine, is drain pump area wet/leaking? Dried water residue indicates previous leaks.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard**: Water leaking toward electrical outlets or motor creates electrocution risk. If water reaches electrical components, unplug machine immediately.

**Water Damage**: Prolonged leak damages floors, walls, substructure. Address quickly.

**Slipping Hazard**: Wet floor is slipping hazard.

## When To Call A Technician
- Cracked hose requiring replacement
- Door seal torn/cracked (requires replacement)
- Leak from pump area (pump seal failure)
- Crack in drum suspected (serious failure)
- Leak from internal connections
- Water level sensor overflow (constant water production)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Inspect inlet/drain hoses annually
- Test door seal closure quarterly
- Do not pull or stress hoses during operation
- Ensure machine is level (uneven placement stresses seals)
- Use correct hose connections (match tap type)
- Replace hoses every 5 years as preventive measure
- Don't expose hoses to extreme heat
