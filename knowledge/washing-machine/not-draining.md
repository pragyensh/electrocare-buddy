# Washing Machine Not Draining

## Overview
Water remains in drum after cycle completes. Clothes soaked with dirty water. Cannot proceed to spin cycle. Caused by clogged drain pump, blocked drain hose, jammed filter, or pump motor failure. Creates water backup and foul smell.

## Symptoms
- Water pooling in drum after cycle
- Clothes very wet and heavy
- Drain pump running but water not draining
- Gurgling sound from drain area
- Slow drainage (taking 15+ minutes to drain)
- Pump motor making noise but no water movement
- Water backing up into sink/floor
- Error code E2 or drain-related code

## Possible Causes
**Clogged Drain Pump**: Pump inlet blocked by lint, coins, or debris. Water cannot enter pump.

**Kinked or Blocked Drain Hose**: Hose bent or obstructed. Water cannot flow out despite pump running.

**Faulty Pump Motor**: Motor doesn't rotate or runs slowly. Cannot create suction to remove water.

**Jammed Pump Filter**: Small filter screen (often front access) clogged with debris.

**Drain Pipe Below Machine**: Home drain pipe blocked (issue with building plumbing, not machine).

**Pump Solenoid Valve Stuck**: Valve prevents water flow despite pump trying to work.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Locate pump filter**: Many machines have front-access filter (bottom front, behind small panel). Open panel. Is filter clogged with lint/debris? Remove and clean thoroughly.

2. **Check drain hose**: Trace plastic hose from machine to drain. Any kinks or bends? Straighten if possible. Is outlet submerged in water (standing water in sink)? Raise outlet above water level.

3. **Listen to pump**: When drain cycle runs, pump should make distinct humming/buzzing sound. No sound = pump not working. Hum with no drainage = pump not creating pressure.

4. **Feel hose**: Is hose warm or moving? Warm hose with no water flow = pump running but blockage present.

5. **Check drain outlet**: Where hose exits machine or drain point, is outlet clear? Try pushing compressed air or water through outlet (unplugged machine).

6. **Manual drain**: If pump isn't working, machine should have emergency drain valve (usually bottom front, small cap). Unscrew cap. Let gravity drain water into bucket. This confirms pump failure if water drains this way.

7. **Inspect hose": Remove drain hose (may require unscrewing or unclipping). Hold up to light. Can you see through clearly? If not, blockage present. Pour water through hose—does it flow freely?

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Pump motor operates at 230V. Do not touch motor while powered.

**Water Damage**: Standing water in machine can damage nearby areas. Drain quickly to prevent floor damage.

**Contamination**: Dirty water in drum is contaminated. Wear gloves when handling.

## When To Call A Technician
- Drain pump making noise but water not draining (pump failure likely)
- Home drain pipe confirmed blocked (requires plumber)
- Pump solenoid valve failure suspected
- Hose has internal blockage that cannot be cleared
- Emergency drain valve broken or stuck

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean drain pump filter every 10-15 washes
- Empty pockets of all clothes before washing (prevent coins/debris from jamming pump)
- Inspect drain hose quarterly for kinks
- Run vinegar cycle monthly to clear mineral buildup
- Don't overfill machine (reduces pump load)
- Use appropriate detergent amount (excess suds block drainage)
