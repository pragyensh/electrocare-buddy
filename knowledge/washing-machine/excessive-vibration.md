# Washing Machine Excessive Vibration

## Overview
Machine vibrates violently during spin cycle. Can move across floor or shift position. Caused by unbalanced load, worn drum bearings, loose drum, or imbalanced weight distribution. Creates noise disturbance and can damage machine structure.

## Symptoms
- Machine rocks side to side during spin
- Violent shaking/bouncing
- Banging noise during spin cycle
- Machine moves position on floor
- Rattling from drum area
- Squealing or grinding during spin
- Water splashing out despite door closed
- Machine stops cycle due to vibration

## Possible Causes
**Unbalanced Load**: Clothes bunched on one side of drum. Weight uneven.

**Worn Drum Bearing**: Bearing allows drum lateral movement instead of perfect rotation.

**Loose Drum Bolt**: Drum bolt fasteners loosened from vibration. Drum tilts during spin.

**Machine Not Level**: Machine feet not adjusted. Vibration resonates at certain speeds.

**Foreign Object Stuck**: Coin, bone, or debris stuck between drum and outer tub. Causes impact during rotation.

**Damaged Drum**: Drum out of round or cracked. Causes imbalance.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check load distribution**: Stop machine. Open drum. Are clothes bunched on one side? Redistribute evenly around drum. Restart spin cycle. Less vibration = load was issue.

2. **Test machine level**: Place spirit level on top of machine. Is it level front-to-back and side-to-side? If tilted, adjust feet underneath machine to level. Most machines have adjustable feet.

3. **Remove foreign objects**: Inspect area between drum and outer tub (accessible from top or front after removing panels). Any visible coins, wire, or debris? Remove carefully (may be sharp).

4. **Reduce load volume**: If you're filling machine to maximum capacity, try reducing to 2/3 capacity. Lighter load may reduce vibration.

5. **Check drum bolts": Look for visible drum bolts (usually accessible from back after removing panel). Are they tight or loose? If you can see bolts and they appear loose, machine needs servicing.

6. **Feel for lateral movement": Stop machine with door open. Try pushing drum side to side with hand. Should be no lateral movement. Any play = bearing wear.

7. **Test different cycles": Run gentle/normal cycle first. Does it vibrate? Then try spin-only cycle. If gentle cycle is fine but spin violent, bearing/balance issue likely.

## Safety Warnings
**Structural Damage**: Prolonged vibration can damage building structure, especially on upper floors. Address quickly.

**Noise Hazard**: Excessive vibration creates loud noise. Hearing protection may be needed near machine.

**Entanglement": Keep hands away from vibrating machine.

## When To Call A Technician
- Machine vibrates even with balanced/reduced load
- Drum moves laterally when pushed (bearing failure)
- Loud grinding noise during spin (bearing or foreign object)
- Machine rocks violently despite leveling attempts
- Drum bolts loose (requires adjustment/replacement)

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Machine leveling/foot adjustment: ₹300–₹600
- Foreign object removal: ₹500–₹1000
- Drum bearing replacement: ₹2500–₹5000
- Drum bolt tightening: ₹600–₹1200
- Complete drum assembly: ₹4000–₹8000

## Preventive Maintenance
- Distribute load evenly always (never skip step)
- Check machine level quarterly
- Ensure machine feet rest on all contact points
- Place machine on level surface (no floor slope)
- Empty all pockets (prevent debris in drum)
- Don't overfill machine
- Use appropriate wash cycles (gentler for lighter loads)
