# Microwave Burning Smell

## Overview
Burnt odor coming from microwave during operation. Indicates component overheating, arcing, motor burnout, or food residue burning. Potential fire hazard. Must investigate cause immediately.

## Symptoms
- Burnt smell during microwave operation
- Smell continues after stopping microwave
- Burnt plastic odor
- Burnt food smell
- Acrid/electrical burnt smell
- Visible smoke inside microwave
- Charred food or debris visible
- Magnetron area very hot

## Possible Causes
**Burnt Food Residue": Accumulated burnt food on interior surfaces. Heating causes burnt smell.

**Overheated Magnetron": Magnetron tube running too hot or too long. Can burn internal components.

**Motor Overheating": Turntable or cooling motor overheated from overuse or bearing failure.

**Burnt Electrical Component": Capacitor, transformer, or other electrical component overheated/arcing.

**Arcing in Waveguide": Damaged waveguide cover causing electrical arcing, burning smell.

**Thermal Fuse Blowing": Safety fuse burns when overheated. Produces burnt smell.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Stop immediately": When burnt smell detected, press stop. Do not continue operating.

2. **Open door and inspect": Look inside. Any visible burned food, debris, or charred areas? Remove if safe (no electrical contact).

3. **Wipe interior": Use damp cloth to clean interior thoroughly. Remove all burnt residue. Air dry completely.

4. **Check exterior": Is exterior very hot to touch? Should be warm but not untouchable. Very hot exterior = internal overheating.

5. **Wait and cool": Allow microwave to cool for 30 minutes. Do not operate again immediately.

6. **Smell after cooling": After cooling, do you still smell burnt? If smell gone, was food residue. If smell persists, component issue.

7. **Inspect turntable motor area": Look at motor area (bottom front under turntable) for any visible burns, blackening, or damage. Smell strongest there? Motor likely overheated.

## Safety Warnings
**Fire Hazard": Burnt smell may indicate fire risk. Do not ignore. Do not leave unattended if continuing to smell burnt.

**Smoke Inhalation": Burnt smell can be toxic. Ventilate room well.

**Electrical Fire": Arcing or component burning creates electrical fire risk.

## When To Call A Technician
- Burnt smell persists after cleaning (component issue)
- Visible burn damage inside microwave
- Burnt smell accompanied by smoke
- Motor area shows burn marks
- Burnt smell after minimal use (early warning sign)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean interior weekly (prevent burnt residue accumulation)
- Don't overheat microwave (use appropriate power levels)
- Don't run microwave empty
- Allow cool-down between heavy use
- Use cover for foods that splatter
- Have annual inspection for older microwaves (>5 years)
