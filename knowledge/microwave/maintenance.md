# Microwave Maintenance

## Overview
Routine care to maintain microwave efficiency, safety, and extend operational lifespan. Well-maintained microwave operates 10-15 years; neglected units fail in 5-7 years.

## Weekly Maintenance

**Clean Interior": After cooking, wipe interior with damp cloth. Remove any food splatters, grease, or liquid. Wipe turntable. Allow interior to dry completely.

**Clean Turntable": Remove turntable. Wash with warm soapy water. Dry completely before reinstalling. Prevents food residue buildup.

**Wipe Door Glass": Wipe door glass interior and exterior with damp cloth. Clean glass improves light penetration and visibility.

## Monthly Maintenance

**Deep Interior Cleaning": Fill bowl with water mixed with lemon juice or vinegar. Microwave on high 5 minutes. Steam loosens buildup. Wipe thoroughly.

**Inspect Waveguide Cover": Look at waveguide cover opening (usually at top inside). Any visible debris or damage? Clean very gently with slightly damp cloth (do not insert objects).

**Check Cooling Vents": Inspect vents on back/sides of microwave. Any dust blockage? Use soft brush to gently remove dust (prevents overheating).

**Test Door Seal": Close door on paper. Pull paper. Should have resistance. Easy removal indicates seal wear—note for future replacement.

## Quarterly Maintenance

**Run Vinegar Cycle": Fill bowl with vinegar and water (1:1). Microwave on high 5-10 minutes. Steam clears mineral deposits and odors. Wipe completely.

**Inspect Power Cord": Check plug and cord for damage, fraying, or burn marks. Replace if damaged.

**Test Door Switch": Open and close door. Should click distinctly. Multiple opening/closing—listen for consistency. Inconsistent = switch wear.

## Annual Maintenance

**Professional Inspection": Have technician check:
- Magnetron condition
- Turntable motor operation
- Cooling fan operation
- Electrical connections
- Capacitor condition
- Overall safety

**Replace Turntable if Needed": After 8-10 years of use, turntable may develop cracks or surface damage. Consider preventive replacement.

## When To Replace Components

**Door Seal": Every 8-10 years (if visibly worn)

**Turntable": Every 8-10 years (or if cracked)

**Motor": 10+ years (or if making noise)

## Safety Checks

**Never Operate With Door Open": Door safety switch must work. Test monthly—opening door should stop operation.

**Inspect for Damage": Look for cracks, dents, or loose panels. Stop using if damage visible.

**Check for Water Leaks": Water in electrical components = hazard. If water inside, unplug and dry completely.

**Verify Grounding": Ensure outlet properly grounded. Use outlet tester.

## Electrical Safety

**Use Voltage Stabilizer": Install 1-2 kVA stabilizer to protect from voltage fluctuations. Prevents control board damage.

**Dedicated Outlet**: Don't share outlet with high-power appliances. Use dedicated outlet with its own breaker.

**Regular Inspection": Plug and cord should show no damage. Replace cord if fraying visible.

## Food Safety During Cooking

**Stir Halfway": Open microwave halfway through cooking. Stir or rearrange food for even heating. Prevents cold spots where bacteria survives.

**Use Microwave-Safe Containers": Only ceramic, glass, or microwave-safe plastics. Never use metal or regular plastics.

**Cover Food": Use microwave cover or paper towel to prevent splatters. Reduces cleaning and prevents buildup.

**Allow Standing Time": After cooking, let food rest 1-2 minutes. Heat distributes evenly. Reduces risk of burns.

## Operational Guidelines to Extend Life

**Appropriate Power Level": Use high power only for water/beverages. Use medium power for most foods. Low power for reheating. Correct power level reduces motor strain.

**Don't Run Empty": Never operate empty microwave. Always have food/water inside. Magnetron needs load.

**Avoid Continuous Running": Microwave for short periods (30 seconds - 5 minutes typically). Allow brief cooldowns between operations.

**Don't Interrupt Cycles": Let cycle complete naturally. Stopping suddenly stresses components.

**Prevent Moisture Inside": After using, leave door open 15 minutes to allow interior drying. Prevents mold and electrical corrosion.

## Cost Savings Summary

**Prevention Investment**: ₹100-300/month for cleaning supplies

**Potential Savings**: ₹1500-4000/year in avoided repairs

**Lifespan Extension**: Proper maintenance adds 3-5 years to microwave life

**Energy Efficiency**: Well-maintained microwave uses 5-10% less energy

## Estimated Annual Maintenance Cost

- Cleaning supplies: ₹200-400
- Professional inspection: ₹1000-1500 annually
- Replacement parts (averaged per year): ₹200-500
- **Total Average**: ₹1400-2400 per year

## Emergency Procedures

**Microwave Won't Stop": Unplug immediately. Do not attempt to fix.

**Fire Inside Microwave": Do not open door. Unplug immediately. Allow fire to suffocate inside. Door stays closed. Call fire service if necessary.

**Electrical Smell": Unplug immediately. Do not use. Professional inspection required.

**Radiation Concern": Never operate with damaged door or cracked waveguide cover.
