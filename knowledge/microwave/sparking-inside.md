# Microwave Sparking Inside

## Overview
Visible sparks/arcing inside microwave during operation. Indicates electrical arc (short circuit) in magnetron chamber or waveguide. Risk of fire and magnetron damage. Must stop operation immediately.

## Symptoms
- Visible electrical sparks inside microwave
- Blue/white arcing visible in cooking chamber
- Burning smell
- Popping/crackling sounds
- Food smoking or catching fire
- Magnetron damage risk
- Fire hazard inside microwave
- Usually occurs with metal objects

## Possible Causes
**Metal Inside Microwave": Most common. Forks, knives, aluminum foil, metal container edges. Metal reflects microwaves and causes arcing.

**Damaged Waveguide Cover": Protective cover over magnetron opening cracked or broken. Exposes internal components.

**Food with High Mineral Content": Sometimes dense foods with minerals arc, but rare.

**Arcing in Magnetron": Internal magnetron defect causes sparking.

**Accumulation of Food Particles": Burnt-on food inside waveguide area conducts electricity, causing arcs.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Stop immediately": When sparking occurs, press stop button. Do not let microwave continue running. Open door.

2. **Remove metal objects": Check for any metal items inside. Forks, spoons, metal foil, metal containers. Remove all metal.

3. **Inspect food container": Is container metal-rimmed or have metal decorations? Use ceramic or microwave-safe plastic only.

4. **Check for aluminum foil": Food wrapped in aluminum foil? Remove foil. Use plastic wrap instead.

5. **Clean waveguide area": If accessible from front, look at waveguide opening (usually covered). Any burnt residue? Clean very gently with damp cloth (do not scrub). Do not insert objects into waveguide.

6. **Test without metal": Place safe food (non-metal container) in microwave. Run 30 seconds. Any sparking? If no sparking, problem was metal.

7. **Inspect interior": Look for visible damage to waveguide cover. Any cracks or broken pieces?

## Safety Warnings
**Fire Hazard": Sparking can ignite food or cabinet interior. Always monitor microwave.

**Microwave Exposure": Never operate with door open or damaged waveguide cover. Radiation hazard.

**Electrical Risk": Do not attempt to repair electrical components inside. Seek professional service.

## When To Call A Technician
- Sparking persists without metal items inside
- Waveguide cover cracked or damaged
- Burnt smell continues after cleaning
- Arcing from magnetron itself (internal issue)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Never put metal in microwave (forks, spoons, foil, metal containers)
- Use microwave-safe containers only
- Remove metal decorations from containers before microwaving
- Don't microwave aluminum foil
- Clean interior after spills immediately
- Inspect waveguide cover monthly for cracks
- Use cover or paper towel to catch splatters (prevents accumulation)
