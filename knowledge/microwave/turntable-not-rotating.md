# Microwave Turntable Not Rotating

## Overview
Microwave heats but turntable doesn't rotate. Food cooks unevenly—overcooked on bottom, undercooked on sides. Caused by broken turntable motor, misaligned turntable, or electrical fault in motor circuit.

## Symptoms
- Turntable stationary during microwave run
- Humming noise but no rotation
- Turntable off its track
- Food heats unevenly
- Click or buzzing sound from turntable area
- Microwave otherwise functions normally
- Smoke/burning smell from turntable area

## Possible Causes
**Failed Turntable Motor": Motor has stopped working. Cannot rotate turntable.

**Faulty Motor Capacitor": Capacitor helps motor start. Failure prevents rotation (motor hums but no spin).

**Turntable Stuck/Jammed": Object under turntable prevents rotation. Food debris or glass shard.

**Misaligned Turntable": Turntable off its support track. Cannot rotate even if motor working.

**Burned Motor Connector": Electrical connection to motor corroded or burnt.

**Thermal Overload": Motor overheated, thermal switch cut power to motor.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Stop microwave": Press stop. Open door. Don't reach into running microwave.

2. **Remove turntable": Lift turntable out carefully. Look underneath. Any debris, food fragments, or glass pieces? Remove gently.

3. **Inspect turntable base": Is turntable base (plastic track that goes on spindle) cracked or broken? Can you spin it by hand? Should spin smoothly.

4. **Check spindle": Center spindle should be clean and not bent. Wipe if debris visible.

5. **Reinstall turntable": Place turntable back on spindle carefully. Ensure it seats properly on support track. Should sit level.

6. **Test rotation": Close door. Run microwave 10 seconds. Does turntable rotate? If yes, problem solved (was misalignment or debris).

7. **Listen to motor": If turntable still doesn't rotate, listen to turntable area during microwave run. Humming = motor working but something stuck. No sound = motor not working.

8. **Check for burning": Smell around turntable area. Burnt smell = motor overheated or connector burned. Stop using.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical": Do not touch turntable motor directly. Electrical hazard.

**Uneven Heating": Operating without rotating turntable is safe but food heats unevenly. Hot spots can cause burns.

## When To Call A Technician
- Turntable motor not running (confirmed no humming)
- Motor connector visibly burnt
- Turntable base cracked
- Thermal overload triggered repeatedly

## Preventive Maintenance
- Keep turntable clean (remove spills promptly)
- Don't place turntable back incorrectly (ensure seated on support)
- Don't microwave turntable if cracked
- Allow motor cool-down between heavy-use sessions
- Inspect turntable base quarterly for cracks
