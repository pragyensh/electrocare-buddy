# Microwave Not Heating

## Overview
Microwave powers on, display shows time/functions, but food doesn't heat during cooking. Magnetron (heating component) has failed or power to magnetron blocked. Food remains cold despite cycle running.

## Symptoms
- Microwave runs for set time but food cold after
- Nothing heats whatsoever
- Turntable spins normally
- Display functions work
- No sparking or unusual sounds
- Exterior warm (normal) but interior not heating

## Possible Causes
**Failed Magnetron**: Magnetron tube produces microwave energy. If failed, no heating occurs. Most common cause.

**Faulty High Voltage Capacitor**: Capacitor stores high voltage for magnetron. If failed, insufficient voltage to magnetron.

**Broken High Voltage Diode**: Diode converts AC to DC for magnetron circuit. Failure prevents voltage supply.

**Burnt Thermal Fuse": Safety device that cuts power if magnetron overheats. If blown, magnetron disconnected.

**Failed Transformer": Transformer steps up power supply voltage for magnetron circuit. Failure prevents high voltage generation.

**Faulty Door Switch": Door safety switch prevents magnetron power when door open. Stuck switch may cut power.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Test with room temperature water**: Put cup of water in microwave. Run on high 1 minute. Feel cup bottom (careful—might be hot). Cold = not heating.

2. **Check turntable": Does turntable spin during microwave run? Turntable motor separate from magnetron. Working turntable + no heat = magnetron issue likely.

3. **Listen for sounds": During heating, should hear humming sound (magnetron and transformer). No humming = power not reaching magnetron circuit.

4. **Test lights": Does interior light turn on when door opens? Light working + no heating = separate circuits (magnetron circuit failed).

5. **Check door switch": Close door firmly. Look for small button on door frame (where door pushes). Press it with finger. Light should turn off (if it's working). If no response, door switch may be stuck.

6. **Feel transformer": Open microwave and observe transformer (large metal box inside—requires professional observation, but check from outside if visible). Is it warm/hot? No heat = not operating.

## Safety Warnings
**High Voltage": Microwave capacitor retains dangerous voltage even when unplugged. Professional service recommended.

**Magnetron Radiation": Never operate microwave with door open or with holes in waveguide cover. Microwave radiation hazard.

**Thermal Risk": Magnetron gets very hot. Can cause burns.

## When To Call A Technician
- Confirmed not heating (water test)
- No humming sound during operation
- Thermal fuse blown (requires replacement)
- Magnetron failure suspected

## Preventive Maintenance
- Don't run microwave empty (overheats magnetron)
- Use turntable always (ensures even heating)
- Clean interior monthly (prevent food residue buildup)
- Allow cooling between heavy use sessions
- Use microwave-safe containers only
