# Microwave Unusual Noise

## Overview
Abnormal sounds coming from microwave during operation. Squealing, grinding, buzzing, or rattling. Indicates mechanical wear, motor problems, or component failure. Noise may increase over time if issue progresses.

## Symptoms
- High-pitched squealing sound
- Grinding noise during operation
- Loud buzzing from motor area
- Rattling from turntable
- Popping or crackling sounds
- Humming louder than normal
- Mechanical clicking from timer/latch

## Possible Causes
**Worn Turntable Motor Bearing": Bearing degrades, produces squealing or grinding.

**Faulty Transformer": Transformer vibrates excessively, produces humming/buzzing.

**Cooling Fan Deterioration": Exhaust fan blade loose or bearing worn. Grinding noise.

**Turntable Misalignment": Turntable wobbles on spindle. Rattling during rotation.

**High Voltage Capacitor Issue": Capacitor may buzz if failing.

**Motor Bearing Wear": Turntable or exhaust motor bearing at end of life.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Identify noise type": Squealing, grinding, buzzing, or rattling? Different sounds indicate different issues.

2. **Identify when noise occurs": During heating only? Only during turntable rotation? Constant? Timing narrows diagnosis.

3. **Check turntable": Remove turntable. Does noise occur? If yes, motor or transformer issue (not turntable). If no, turntable misaligned.

4. **Reinstall turntable correctly": If turntable was removed, ensure it's seated properly on spindle and support track.

5. **Listen to motor area": During operation, hold ear near turntable motor area (bottom). Squealing = bearing wear. Humming = normal.

6. **Check for loose components": Gently shake microwave. Any loose rattling inside (not from turntable)? Might be loose transformer or bracket.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical": Do not attempt to repair motor or transformer. Seek professional service.

**Continuing Damage": Operating with excessive noise may accelerate component wear.

## When To Call A Technician
- Squealing/grinding from turntable area (motor bearing)
- Buzzing from transformer area
- Grinding sound (fan blade issue)
- Continuous loud noise

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Turntable motor replacement: ₹1200–₹2500
- Transformer replacement: ₹2000–₹4000
- Cooling fan replacement: ₹1500–₹3000
- Motor bearing lubrication: ₹600–₹1200

## Preventive Maintenance
- Monitor noise level regularly
- Reduce microwave usage if noise develops (avoid accelerating damage)
- Clean cooling vents (prevent fan strain)
- Allow microwave cool-down between sessions
- Have inspection annually if microwave >5 years old
