# Refrigerator Door Not Closing

## Overview
Refrigerator door doesn't seal properly. Gap visible or door swings open easily. Allows warm air to enter, causes compressor to overwork, and spoils food. Caused by misaligned door, worn hinges, bent door frame, or faulty magnetic catch.

## Symptoms
- Door swings open on its own
- Visible gap between door and frame
- Door hard to close or doesn't latch
- Food spoils quickly
- Compressor running constantly
- Fridge not cold despite unit operating
- Sound of air escaping when door is "closed"
- Frost/condensation inside fridge

## Possible Causes
**Misaligned Door**: Hinges loose or door shifted. Door no longer parallel to frame.

**Worn Rubber Seal**: Seal doesn't maintain compression, allowing air leakage.

**Bent Door Frame**: Frame warped, prevents door from seating properly.

**Faulty Magnetic Catch**: Magnet in door or frame weakened or broken. Door doesn't hold closed.

**Loose Hinges**: Door hinges have loose bolts/screws. Door sags or shifts.

**Damaged Latch**: Mechanical latch not engaging properly.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check door seal**: Close door on dollar bill. Try pulling bill out. Should be slight resistance. If bill slides out easily, seal is worn.

2. **Inspect rubber seal**: Look at rubber gasket around door edge. Any visible tears, cracks, or flat spots?

3. **Check door alignment**: Close door. Look at gap around frame. Should be even on all sides (approximately same size). Uneven gap suggests misalignment.

4. **Test magnetic catch**: Open door. Feel for magnet in door edge and frame. Magnets should attract noticeably. Weak attraction indicates weak magnet.

5. **Inspect hinges**: Look at door hinges (top and bottom). Are bolts/screws tight or loose? Loose hinges cause door sag.

6. **Check door for physical damage**: Is door bent, cracked, or warped? Close door against flat surface and look for gaps.

7. **Try adjusting hinges**: If safe and within limits, top hinge may have adjustment screw to raise/lower door alignment. Consult manual before attempting.

8. **Clean seal**: Debris on rubber seal may prevent proper closing. Wipe seal with vinegar solution and dry.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Do not attempt to access electrical components in door (light switch, solenoid).

**Mechanical**: Hinges are under spring tension. Be cautious when adjusting.

## When To Call A Technician
- Door alignment cannot be adjusted (requires hinge replacement)
- Rubber seal torn or split
- Magnetic catch weak (requires magnet replacement)
- Door frame visibly warped
- Latch mechanism broken
- Door cannot be adjusted even with screw tensioning

## Preventive Maintenance
- Check door closure monthly (dollar bill test)
- Keep hinge bolts tight (check quarterly)
- Avoid slamming door hard
- Replace rubber seal every 5-7 years
- Don't lean heavily on open door
- Keep door hinges clean and dry
