# Refrigerator Bad Smell

## Overview
Foul odor coming from fridge interior. Smell intensifies when opening door. Caused by stagnant water in drain line, mold/mildew growth, spoiled food decomposition, or bacterial accumulation. Can affect food quality and trigger nausea in sensitive individuals.

## Symptoms
- Musty/moldy smell when opening fridge
- Rotten-egg or sulphurous smell
- Smell worse after door closed for hours
- Visible mold on shelves or walls
- Black spots on drain area
- Soggy food containers
- Smell persists despite cleaning

## Possible Causes
**Stagnant Water in Drain Line**: Drain tube under fridge collects water with bacteria. Bacteria decompose organic matter, producing foul smell.

**Mold/Mildew Growth**: Humid environment breeds mold on walls, seals, or shelves.

**Spoiled Food**: Old food or meat decomposing in back of fridge (forgotten items).

**Bacterial Growth**: Various bacteria produce different smells—sulfur, rotten eggs, etc.

**Dirty Drain Pan**: Plastic pan under fridge holds stagnant water with organic matter.

**Faulty Water Dispenser**: Stagnant water in supply line to dispenser develops smell.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Remove all food**: Take out all items. Check for spoiled food, especially in back corners.

2. **Clean interior thoroughly**: Wipe all surfaces with 1:1 vinegar/water solution. Remove all visible mold/stains.

3. **Access drain**: At bottom front of fridge, locate drain hole. Pour 1 cup of hot water down drain using cup or turkey baster.

4. **Flush drain line**: Use plunger or bulb syringe to create pressure in drain system. Flush multiple times to dislodge debris.

5. **Clean drain pan**: If accessible (usually under fridge), remove pan. Empty stagnant water. Wash thoroughly with bleach solution (1:10 dilute).

6. **Wipe drain outlet**: Rear or bottom outlet where drain tube exits. Wipe with cloth and vinegar solution.

7. **Inspect water dispenser line**: If present, flush water line by dispensing water 2-3 minutes until clear.

8. **Leave door open**: After cleaning, leave fridge/freezer doors open 2 hours to dry thoroughly.

9. **Place odor absorber**: Use activated charcoal, baking soda, or commercial fridge deodorizer. Replace weekly.

## Safety Warnings
**Mold Hazard**: Breathing mold spores can trigger allergies and respiratory issues. Use mask while cleaning visible mold.

**Drain Line Bacteria**: Some drain bacteria produce endotoxins. Don't inhale fumes directly.

## When To Call A Technician
- Smell persists after thorough cleaning
- Black mold visible throughout interior (extensive growth)
- Water leak preventing drain access
- Drain line blocked and cannot be cleared
- Smell after water dispenser flushing (internal contamination)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean drain monthly with hot water + vinegar
- Empty and clean drain pan quarterly
- Wipe interior monthly with vinegar solution
- Store food properly to prevent spoilage
- Keep fridge at 4°C (slower decomposition of accidental spoilage)
- Don't keep fridge door open unnecessarily
- Use activated charcoal or baking soda monthly as odor absorber
- Replace water filter in dispenser every 3-6 months
