# Refrigerator Not Cooling

## Overview
Refrigerator runs but doesn't cool. Temperature inside reaches 15-20°C instead of target 4°C. Freezer may be frozen while fridge section warm (evaporator valve issue) or both sections warm (compressor/refrigerant issue). Can cause food spoilage within hours.

## Symptoms
- Fridge interior warm to touch (>15°C)
- Freezer section also warm (not frozen)
- Compressor running continuously but no cooling
- No ice in freezer, water not freezing
- Food spoiling despite unit powered on
- Condenser fan running or not running
- Moisture on fridge walls
- Unusual smell inside unit

## Possible Causes
**Refrigerant Leak**: Small hole in evaporator coil or connection tubing. Refrigerant escapes over weeks. Compressor runs but cannot cool without refrigerant.

**Frozen Evaporator Coil**: Defrost cycle failed. Ice blocks airflow through coil. Normally rare in modern units but occurs with sensor failure.

**Faulty Thermostat**: Temperature sensor in fridge cannot read room temperature. Thermostat doesn't trigger compressor or cycles off prematurely.

**Blocked Condenser Coil**: Dust layer on rear-facing coil prevents heat rejection. Compressor overheats, shuts off thermally.

**Compressor Failure**: Worn internal components or complete failure. Doesn't pressurize refrigerant.

**Faulty Evaporator Fan**: Fan motor not running. Doesn't circulate cold air from evaporator to fridge/freezer compartments.

**Damper Control Valve Stuck**: Valve controls airflow from freezer to fridge. If stuck fully toward freezer, no cold air reaches fridge section.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check power**: Is unit plugged in? Look at back of fridge. Is power cord connected? Check outlet with another device (lamp).

2. **Locate compressor**: At back of fridge, find compressor (metal cylinder, usually near floor). Does it vibrate when unit runs? No vibration = not running or severely weak.

3. **Feel compressor body**: Is it warm (60°C+)? Compressor generates heat during operation. Ice-cold compressor = not running.

4. **Check for frost**: Open freezer. Any frost on freezer coil (looks like radiator, usually back wall)? No frost = refrigerant issue. Excessive frost = defrost failure.

5. **Inspect condenser coil**: At back bottom of fridge (or on side), find condenser coil (looks like radiator). Is it clogged with dust/hair? Gently brush with soft brush. Extreme dust coating blocks heat rejection.

6. **Test thermostat**: Is fridge temperature dial accessible? Try moving to coldest setting, wait 2 hours. Any cooling? If no response to setting change, thermostat may be faulty.

7. **Listen for fan**: Should hear soft humming from evaporator fan inside fridge. No sound = fan not running. Sound from fan but no cooling = fan running but cooled air not circulating.

8. **Check drainpan**: If condensation present in fridge (sweating walls), compressor is running but not cooling sufficiently. If no condensation, compressor likely not running.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Compressor runs on 230V AC or 12V DC (some models). Do not attempt internal repairs.

**Refrigerant**: Leaking refrigerant is harmful. Do not attempt repairs. R600A (used in some fridges) is flammable.

**Mechanical**: Rotating fan and compressor are hazards. Never insert objects into vents.

## When To Call A Technician
- Compressor not vibrating despite power
- Refrigerant leak visible (oily spots inside compressor area)
- Condenser coil extremely clogged (professional vacuum recommended)
- Freezer iced solid but fridge warm (expansion valve failure)
- Thermostat not responding to setting changes
- Evaporator fan not running
- Temperature cannot be maintained despite unit running
- Strange hissing or bubbling sounds (refrigerant leak)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Vacuum condenser coil quarterly (use soft brush, not aggressive suction)
- Ensure fridge is level (check with spirit level quarterly)
- Keep fridge away from walls (minimum 5cm clearance for airflow)
- Replace door seals if cracked/torn (loss of cooling)
- Do not store items directly against back wall (blocks airflow)
- Keep fridge away from direct sunlight
- Allow air circulation behind unit (don't push against wall)
- Avoid storing warm food in fridge (pre-cool to room temp)
- Don't open door frequently or for extended periods
