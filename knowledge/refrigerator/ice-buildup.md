# Refrigerator Ice Buildup

## Overview
Ice accumulation on evaporator coil or interior surfaces. Blocks airflow and prevents proper cooling. Occurs when humidity inside fridge is high or defrost cycle malfunctions. Can completely freeze fridge if unchecked. Defrost heater failure is most common cause.

## Symptoms
- Thick ice layer on back wall of freezer
- Ice buildup on evaporator coil (visible frost)
- Fridge not cooling despite ice accumulation
- Freezer extremely cold (below -20°C) but fridge warm
- Water damage under ice when it melts
- High humidity visible (condensation on shelves)
- Ice on food items

## Possible Causes
**Defrost Heater Failure**: Small heating element in fridge that periodically melts ice from evaporator. If failed, ice accumulates continuously.

**Defrost Thermostat Failure**: Sensor that triggers heater has failed. Heater never activates.

**Blocked Defrost Drain**: Drain cannot handle ice melt volume. Water refreezes on coil.

**Door Not Sealing**: Humid outside air leaks in constantly. Creates condensation that freezes on coil.

**High Humidity Setting**: Fridge humidity set too high. Excessive moisture.

**Temperature Set Too Low**: Fridge set to below 0°C. Moisture freezes instead of draining.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Manual defrost cycle**: Turn fridge OFF. Leave door open for 24 hours. Allow ice to melt naturally. Monitor floor for water. This is temporary fix.

2. **Check door seal**: Is rubber seal around door intact? Close door on paper—should be slight resistance when pulling paper out. If paper slides out easily, seal is broken.

3. **Inspect defrost drain**: Ice buildup may have blocked drain. Locate drain at bottom of freezer. Try to clear with turkey baster.

4. **Check freezer temperature**: Should be -18°C or colder. If actual temp is -5°C despite setting, thermostat may be failing.

5. **Wipe interior moisture**: After defrost, wipe all ice/moisture with soft cloth. Leave door open 2 hours to dry thoroughly.

6. **Don't resume operation until dry**: Running fridge with moisture present causes immediate refreeze.

7. **Reduce humidity if possible**: Open fridge less frequently. Don't store very cold items (they attract condensation).

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Defrost heater operates at 230V AC. Do not touch or attempt to test.

**Mechanical**: Do not attempt to chip ice with sharp objects. Risk of damaging evaporator coil (refrigerant leak).

**Thermal**: After long defrost cycle, fridge is very cold. Allow 30 minutes of normal operation before storing food.

## When To Call A Technician
- Ice returns within 24 hours of manual defrost (heater failure likely)
- Door seal visibly torn or cracked
- Defrost drain cannot be cleared
- Freezer not reaching -18°C despite setting
- Defrost thermostat replacement needed
- Extensive mold visible with ice

## Preventive Maintenance
- Check door seal monthly (should be firm and complete)
- Replace door seal every 5 years
- Don't keep fridge door open for extended periods
- Ensure humid air doesn't leak in through gaps
- Set fridge to 0-4°C, freezer to -18°C (not colder)
- Check defrost heater annually (professional inspection)
- Clean defrost drain quarterly
