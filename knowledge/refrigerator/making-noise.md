# Refrigerator Making Noise

## Overview
Unusual sounds from refrigerator during normal operation. Compressor makes grinding, buzzing, or rattling. Evaporator/condenser fan produces squealing or grinding. Water pump gurgling. Most noises indicate loose components or bearing wear, rarely require immediate action but should be investigated.

## Symptoms
- Loud buzzing or humming from compressor area
- Grinding or squealing from rear/bottom
- Rattling or vibrating noise
- Gurgling sound from water supply line
- Clicking from thermostat or solenoid
- Hissing from refrigerant circulation
- Vibration noise from fridge body
- Noise intensity increases during cooling cycles

## Possible Causes
**Loose Compressor Mounting Bolts**: Compressor vibrates excessively. Causes buzzing/grinding and metal-on-metal rattling.

**Worn Compressor Bearings**: Internal friction produces squealing noise. Indicates bearing wear, progresses to complete failure.

**Condenser/Evaporator Fan Worn**: Fan blade bearings worn. Produces squealing or grinding. May also indicate blade obstruction.

**Frost Blockage in Evaporator**: Ice buildup in evaporator creates airflow turbulence/hissing.

**Refrigerant Flow Noise**: High-speed refrigerant through expansion valve creates normal hissing/gurgling (not necessarily a problem).

**Water Supply Line**: If ice maker present, water line may vibrate or have air bubbles (gurgling).

**Loose Interior Components**: Ice maker parts, shelving, or internal deflectors vibrate if loose.

**Thermostat Solenoid Clicking**: Normal clicking as thermostat engages/disengages. If excessive, solenoid failing.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Identify noise source**: Determine if noise is from compressor (lower back), fan (rear), or inside fridge. Listen carefully with ear near suspected area.

2. **Check compressor bolts**: If accessible (and safe), visually inspect compressor mounting. Bolts should be tight. Do not attempt to tighten without proper knowledge.

3. **Inspect behind fridge**: Move fridge away from wall (unplug first). Check behind/below for debris fallen into fan area. Remove visible debris carefully.

4. **Listen to condenser fan**: Does fan spin freely when unit unplugged and pushed manually? Any grinding indicates bearing damage.

5. **Check interior components**: Open fridge and freezer. Look for loose shelves, drawers, or ice maker parts that might rattle. Tighten if possible.

6. **Measure noise level**: Use phone decibel meter app. Normal operation ~45-55 dB. Loud >70 dB.

7. **Note noise pattern**: Does noise occur only during compressor on-cycle? Or continuously? Compressor-only noise is usually less urgent.

8. **Feel vibration**: Place hand on fridge side. Excessive vibration suggests loose compressor mounting or bearing wear.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Compressor and fan motor operate at 230V AC. Do not attempt internal repairs.

**Mechanical**: Rotating fan is hazard. Never insert objects into vents while plugged in.

**Refrigerant**: Some unusual sounds indicate refrigerant leaks. Do not attempt to fix.

## When To Call A Technician
- Compressor squealing (bearing wear likely progressing to failure)
- Grinding noise from compressor
- Noise accompanied by cooling loss
- Buzzing that continues even after compressor cycles off (electrical issue)
- Loud rattling from interior ice maker (component failure)
- Clicking noise excessively frequent (thermostat failure)
- Hissing with visible frost/ice outside fridge (refrigerant leak)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Keep refrigerator level (uneven placement causes vibration)
- Ensure fridge is on solid ground (not on uneven surface)
- Check that fridge isn't in contact with walls (causes vibration transfer)
- Vacuum around compressor area (dust can rattle when loose)
- Have annual professional inspection to identify early wear
- Listen to fridge monthly and note any changes in sound
- Don't overload fridge with items (reduces vibration)
