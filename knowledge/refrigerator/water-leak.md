# Refrigerator Water Leak

## Overview
Water dripping inside or below refrigerator. Caused by clogged drain line, frozen drain, broken water supply line, or misaligned evaporator pan. Can cause electrical hazard if water reaches power components, mold growth, and damage to adjacent cabinetry.

## Symptoms
- Water pooling at bottom of fridge interior
- Water dripping from ceiling of fridge
- Water leak below refrigerator (floor wet)
- Moisture on fridge walls
- Musty smell (mold growth)
- Ice maker not producing ice (frozen drain)
- Water leaking from rear of fridge
- Soggy food boxes

## Possible Causes
**Clogged Drain Line**: Drain tube underneath fridge collects food particles, dust. Gets blocked. Water backs up into fridge. Most common cause (70%).

**Frozen Drain**: In cold climates or when fridge set too cold, water in drain line freezes. Blocks flow.

**Broken Water Supply Line**: Plastic tube connected to ice maker/water dispenser cracks. Water leaks instead of flowing to ice maker.

**Cracked Evaporator Pan**: Plastic pan under evaporator coil collects condensation. If cracked, water leaks directly onto floor.

**Misaligned Evaporator Pan**: Pan shifted and water misses drain outlet. Water overflows.

**Dirty Defrost Drain Hole**: Small hole in evaporator coil drains condensate. If plugged with ice/debris, water backs up.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Locate defrost drain**: At bottom front of fridge, small hole or slit (typically center). This is main drain. Pour water through it with cup. Water should flow out rear/bottom. If blocked, use turkey baster to clear.

2. **Check rear drain line**: At back of fridge (may need to move fridge), find plastic drain tube. Is it kinked, cracked, or disconnected? Replace or straighten if kinked.

3. **Flush drain line**: Use turkey baster or syringe filled with water. Squirt into defrost drain hole. Observe if water flows out rear. Repeat with warm water if ice suspected.

4. **Inspect evaporator pan**: If accessible (usually under fridge or behind rear panel), check plastic pan. Any visible cracks? Is it properly positioned under drain outlet?

5. **Check water supply line**: If ice maker present, find supply line (usually 1/4-inch plastic tube from back of fridge). Any visible cracks, leaks, or disconnections?

6. **Test ice maker**: If supply line connected, ice maker should dispense water. If no water comes out, line may be blocked or frozen.

7. **Remove buildup**: Clean drain hole area with soft brush. Remove mineral deposits or mold with vinegar solution (1:1 with water).

8. **Check fridge level**: Use spirit level on fridge top. Should tilt slightly toward back/bottom for gravity-fed drain. If tilted wrong way, reposition.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard**: Water leaking toward power cord or plug creates electrocution risk. Do not touch wet electrical connections. Unplug fridge immediately if water reaches power components.

**Mold Growth**: Damp areas breed mold quickly. Can trigger respiratory issues. Clean leaked areas thoroughly with bleach solution after repair.

**Structural Damage**: Prolonged leak causes floor damage, rot, and foundation issues. Address quickly.

## When To Call A Technician
- Water supply line cracked/leaking
- Evaporator pan cracked (replacement needed)
- Defrost drain hole cannot be cleared by flushing
- Water leaking from refrigerant lines (internal leak)
- Extensive mold growth inside fridge
- Electrical damage suspected (water near wiring)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean defrost drain monthly with turkey baster filled with vinegar solution
- Flush drain line quarterly with warm water
- Check that fridge is tilted slightly toward rear (minimum 1/4-inch per foot length)
- Inspect water supply line annually for cracks
- Clean mold/buildup around drain hole
- Ensure drain outlet is not submerged in standing water
- Replace water supply line every 5 years as preventive measure
- In cold climates, insulate drain line to prevent freezing
