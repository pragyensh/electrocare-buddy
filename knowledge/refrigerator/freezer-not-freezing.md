# Refrigerator Freezer Not Freezing

## Overview
Freezer compartment remains at 5-10°C instead of target -18°C. Food doesn't freeze solid. Ice cream melts, ice cubes don't form. Fridge compartment may cool normally (if damper valve stuck toward fridge) or both sections warm (if compressor/refrigerant issue).

## Symptoms
- Freezer items partially frozen or thawed
- Ice cream soft/melted
- No ice cubes forming
- Water instead of ice in ice maker
- Freezer warmer than fridge (reversed)
- Compressor running but freezer not cold
- Frost buildup in freezer but still warm

## Possible Causes
**Damper Valve Stuck**: Valve that controls airflow from freezer to fridge is stuck open toward fridge. All cold air goes to fridge, none to freezer.

**Evaporator Fan Not Running**: Fan doesn't circulate cold air from evaporator coil to freezer compartment.

**Low Refrigerant**: Compressor cycles but insufficient refrigerant. Both sections warm, not just freezer.

**Thermostat Failure**: Freezer thermostat not calling for cold. Compressor runs on fridge setting only.

**Blocked Evaporator**: Ice buildup blocks coil, reducing cold air production.

**Compressor Weak/Failing**: Doesn't pressurize refrigerant sufficiently.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Compare fridge/freezer temps**: Use food thermometer in both sections. If fridge is 4°C and freezer 5-8°C, damper valve likely stuck toward fridge.

2. **Check airflow**: Open freezer door. Feel air—should be cold. Then open fridge—should feel less cold. If fridge feels colder, damper issue confirmed.

3. **Locate damper**: Usually behind upper back wall of fridge (accessible). Check if it's stuck fully open position. Try gently moving it if accessible (caution: may cause damage).

4. **Listen for evaporator fan**: Inside fridge/freezer, should hear soft humming from fan. No sound = fan not running.

5. **Check for frost**: If freezer is frosted but still warm, compressor is running but refrigerant issue present.

6. **Test compressor**: Feel rear of fridge. Compressor should vibrate. No vibration = not running or very weak.

7. **Clean condenser**: Rear condenser coil may be clogged. Brush with soft brush to improve efficiency.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Thermostat and damper solenoid operate at high voltage. Do not attempt repairs.

**Refrigerant**: Any refrigerant-related repair requires EPA-certified technician.

## When To Call A Technician
- Damper valve stuck (solenoid failure)
- Evaporator fan not running (motor failure)
- Refrigerant leak suspected (frost pattern odd, or oily spots)
- Freezer never reaches -18°C despite unit running
- Thermostat failure suspected
- Compressor weak or not running

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)

- Refrigerant recharge: ₹2000–₹4000
- Compressor replacement: ₹4000–₹10000

## Preventive Maintenance
- Keep freezer door closed as much as possible (reduces frost/icing)
- Set freezer to -18°C (excessive cold doesn't help)
- Ensure evaporator coil is clear of frost
- Have damper valve tested annually
- Check airflow distribution yearly
