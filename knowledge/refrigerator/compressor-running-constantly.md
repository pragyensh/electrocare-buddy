# Refrigerator Compressor Running Constantly

## Overview
Compressor never cycles off. Runs 24/7 without rest periods. Indicates fridge not reaching set temperature despite compressor operating. Causes excessive electricity consumption, accelerates component wear, and suggests cooling efficiency loss or thermostat malfunction.

## Symptoms
- Compressor vibration/humming continuous (never stops)
- Electricity bill significantly higher
- Fridge still not cool enough despite constant running
- Compressor very hot (>70°C)
- Audible compressor noise all day/night
- Food not cooling to proper temperature
- Unit overheating shutdown cycle may occur
- Condensation excessive inside fridge

## Possible Causes
**Refrigerant Leak**: Gradual leak reduces cooling capacity. Compressor works continuously trying to maintain temperature.

**Dirty Condenser Coil**: Dust/dirt blocks heat rejection. Compressor cannot efficiently reduce pressure. Works harder/longer.

**Faulty Thermostat**: Thermostat doesn't sense target temperature reached. Never signals compressor to stop.

**Weak Compressor**: Worn internal components don't pressurize efficiently. Must run longer to cool.

**Undersized Refrigerator**: Unit too small for kitchen placement (near heat source or in hot climate).

**Door Not Sealing**: Warm air constantly leaks in. Compressor never stops working to overcome heat gain.

**Evaporator Fan Not Running**: Fan doesn't circulate cold air to fridge. Temperature sensors don't read correct temperature.

**High Ambient Temperature**: Fridge placed in hot kitchen or direct sun. High external load.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Measure total runtime**: Set timer for 2 hours. Count how many minutes compressor is ON. Should be 40-50 minutes per hour during normal operation. If >55 minutes, issue exists.

2. **Check door seal**: Is rubber seal around door intact? Close door on paper. Should be slight resistance pulling paper out. If easy to pull, seal is broken.

3. **Feel condenser coil**: Rear of fridge should have coil (looks like radiator). Is it clogged with dust/hair? Gently brush with soft brush.

4. **Check thermostat**: Is temp dial in correct position? Try moving slightly warmer. Wait 30 minutes. Does compressor cycle off? If yes, thermostat adjusted to coldest setting inappropriately.

5. **Measure interior temperature**: Use food thermometer. Should read 4°C in fridge, -18°C in freezer. Higher temps despite constant running suggest thermostat issue or compressor weakness.

6. **Check for frost**: Excessive frost means compressor is cooling but can't distribute air properly (fan issue).

7. **Measure ambient temperature**: Is room temperature >28°C? Very hot external temp increases runtime significantly.

8. **Inspect for leaks**: Look for oily spots near compressor or tubing (refrigerant leak indicator).

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Constant compressor operation increases heat and electrical load. Fire/overheating risk if issue persists.

**Thermal**: Compressor gets very hot (>70°C). Burns easily. Don't touch when running.

## When To Call A Technician
- Compressor running >55 minutes per hour continuously
- Fridge temperature cannot be maintained despite constant running
- Door seal visibly damaged
- Condenser extremely clogged (professional vacuum recommended)
- Refrigerant leak suspected (oily spots)
- Thermostat not responding to temperature adjustments
- Electricity consumption doubled despite same food load

## Preventive Maintenance
- Replace door seal every 5 years
- Clean condenser coil quarterly
- Ensure fridge is away from heat sources (stove, oven, direct sunlight)
- Keep fridge at least 10cm from wall for proper ventilation
- Monitor runtime monthly (should cycle 40-50 min per hour)
- Test thermostat annually
- Maintain proper temperature setting (4°C fridge, -18°C freezer)
