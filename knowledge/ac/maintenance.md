# AC Maintenance

## Overview
Regular maintenance prevents 80% of AC failures and ensures optimal cooling efficiency. Professional maintenance every 6 months and DIY monthly checks extend unit lifespan from 8-10 years to 12-15 years. Maintenance reduces electricity bills by 15-20% and improves indoor air quality.

## Maintenance Schedule

### Weekly (Summer)
- Visually inspect outdoor unit for debris/birds
- Check that drainage water is flowing (pouring water into indoor drain line)
- Listen for unusual sounds during operation
- Feel that outdoor fan is running

### Monthly (Summer)
- Replace or clean air filter (dirty filter = 20% efficiency loss)
- Check indoor unit for water leakage
- Wipe IR receiver window on indoor unit with soft cloth
- Verify thermostat is not blocked by furniture
- Check that return air vents are not blocked

### Quarterly (Every 3 months)
- Professional condenser coil cleaning (removes dust/pollen)
- Visual inspection of refrigerant lines for oil leaks
- Check that electrical connections are tight
- Test AC cooling rate (should drop room temp 1°C per 15-20 minutes)

### Bi-Annual (Every 6 months)
- Professional evaporator coil cleaning (internal unit)
- Refrigerant charge verification (requires technician with gauges)
- Capacitor condition assessment
- Thermostat calibration check
- Drain line deep cleaning with chemical treatment
- Complete electrical inspection

### Annual
- Compressor oil condition check (may need change)
- Pressures verification (high/low side gauges)
- Voltage supply stability check
- Defrost cycle testing (if heat pump model)
- Filter-drier replacement (if accessible)
- UV lamp replacement (if unit has ionizer/UV)
- Professional report and recommendations

## DIY Maintenance Tasks

### Air Filter Replacement
1. Turn off AC
2. Open front panel of indoor unit
3. Locate white/blue filter (typically 30cm×20cm×4cm)
4. Slide out filter noting arrow direction
5. Inspect filter—if >50% gray/black, replace
6. Install new filter with arrow pointing toward unit
7. Close panel

Frequency: Every 15-20 days during cooling season

### Outdoor Unit Cleaning
1. Turn off main breaker
2. Remove debris (leaves, plastic, branches) from intake/outlet
3. Use soft brush to gently brush condenser coil fins from top to bottom
4. Rinse with garden hose at low pressure (never high-pressure wash, damages fins)
5. Allow to dry 30 minutes before restarting

Frequency: Monthly during cooling season

### Drain Line Flushing
1. Locate drain line outlet (usually outside, plastic tube near indoor unit)
2. Use garden hose to gently flush line from outdoor end
3. Pour water slowly; observe flow from indoor drain inlet
4. If slow/blocked, use plumbing snake or call technician
5. Apply dilute bleach (1:10) to reduce algae/bacteria

Frequency: Monthly during cooling season; bimonthly during humidity

### Evaporator Coil Inspection
1. Turn off AC
2. Open indoor unit front panel carefully
3. Look at coil surface (looks like metal fins)
4. Check for black spots (mold) or excessive dust
5. If black spots visible, apply dilute bleach solution and wipe
6. If heavy dust, use soft brush gently
7. Never apply high-pressure water to coil

Frequency: Monthly inspection; professional cleaning every 4 months

## Professional Maintenance Tasks
(Requires licensed AC technician)

### Condenser Coil Chemical Cleaning
- Apply professional coil cleaner (foam)
- Let sit 15-20 minutes
- Rinse with low-pressure water
- Cost: ₹1000–₹2500
- Frequency: Every 3-4 months

### Evaporator Coil Acid Wash
- Applies acid solution to dissolve sludge buildup
- Flushes refrigerant lines
- Cost: ₹1500–₹3500
- Frequency: Every 6-12 months

### Refrigerant Charge Verification
- Uses manifold gauges to measure high/low-side pressure
- Compares to nameplate specification
- Adds refrigerant if charge is low
- Cost: ₹1500–₹4000
- Frequency: Annually

### Compressor Oil Change
- Drains old compressor oil (may contain sludge/moisture)
- Refills with fresh ISO 68 compressor oil
- Cost: ₹1500–₹3000
- Frequency: Every 2-3 years

### Capacitor Replacement
- Tests start and run capacitors for voltage rating
- Replaces if capacity degraded
- Prevents compressor starting issues
- Cost: ₹600–₹1500
- Frequency: Every 3-4 years

### Thermostat Calibration
- Compares thermostat reading to actual room temperature
- Adjusts sensor if misaligned
- Cost: ₹500–₹1200
- Frequency: Annually

## Maintenance Cost Optimization

### Annual Maintenance Budget
- DIY tasks (filters, cleaning): ₹500–₹1000
- Professional quarterly cleaning: ₹1200–₹3000 (₹300–₹750 per visit)
- Annual refrigerant check: ₹1500–₹2000
- Annual electrical inspection: ₹800–₹1500
- Total: ₹4000–₹7500 per year

### Cost-Benefit Analysis
- No maintenance: Unit fails after 8 years. Repair cost ₹15000–₹30000
- Regular maintenance: Unit lasts 12-15 years. Maintenance cost ₹50000–₹75000 over lifespan
- Savings: ₹25000–₹50000 in replacement costs + 15-20% energy savings per year

## Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Professional Service
- Visible oil leaks from compressor
- Any refrigerant hissing
- Grinding or knocking noise
- Compressor not starting (repeated clicking)
- Room not cooling below 26°C after 2 hours
- Consistent ice formation on coil
- Burning smell from outdoor unit
- Any electrical smoke or sparks

## Seasonal Maintenance Schedule

### Pre-Summer (March-April)
- Complete professional service (coil cleaning + refrigerant check)
- Replace filter with fresh one
- Verify all connections tight
- Test cooling capacity at low load

### During Summer (May-September)
- Weekly: Visual checks
- Monthly: Filter replacement + drain flushing
- 3-Monthly: Professional coil cleaning

### Pre-Monsoon (May-June)
- Ensure drain line is clear
- Check outdoor unit protection from rain
- Install dehumidifier if AC stays idle during rain

### Post-Summer (October-November)
- Final deep clean before idle period
- Refrigerant charge verification
- Electrical inspection
- Capacitor testing

### Winter (December-February)
- Unit idle. Check monthly that no water is leaking
- Do not operate AC unless outdoor temp >16°C
- Keep unit covered from dust

## Lifespan Extension Tips
- Maintain room humidity below 60% (reduces compressor load)
- Set thermostat to 22-24°C (reduces runtime by 30%)
- Close blinds on sunny windows (reduces solar heat by 40%)
- Ensure good room ventilation during AC operation
- Use ceiling fan to circulate cooled air (reduces AC runtime)
- Avoid placing AC on west/south-facing walls
- Install shade cloth outside if outdoor unit exposed to sun
- Use dedicated stabilizer for power protection
- Keep room temperature swings minimal (avoid 35°C to 18°C changes)
