# AC Water Leak

## Overview
Water drips from indoor AC unit, either during operation or after shutdown. Can cause ceiling damage, wall staining, electrical hazard, and mold growth. Caused by clogged drain line, misaligned indoor unit, frozen coil thaw, or cracked drainage pan.

## Symptoms
- Water dripping from indoor unit during operation
- Water pooling below indoor unit
- Wet ceiling/wall below AC location
- Musty smell near indoor unit (mold growth)
- Reduced cooling (frozen coil blocking airflow)
- Hissing sound from drain area
- Water leaking into wall cavity

## Possible Causes
**Clogged Drain Line**: Primary cause (70% of cases). Indoor evaporator coil condenses room moisture. Water drips into drainage pan and flows through plastic drain line to outside. Algae, dust, and debris block line. Water backs up and overflows from pan.

**Misaligned Indoor Unit**: Unit not tilted toward drain. Gravity cannot pull water toward drainage outlet. Installation error or vibration shift over years.

**Cracked or Damaged Drain Pan**: Plastic pan under evaporator coil develops cracks or separation. Water leaks directly through crack instead of flowing to drain outlet.

**Frozen Evaporator Coil**: Ice formation on coil when defrost cycle fails. Ice melts and water volume exceeds drain capacity. Water backs up.

**Poor Drain Line Installation**: Drain line installed with upward slope or not sloped at minimum 1/4-inch per 10 feet toward exterior exit. Creates water pocket.

**Faulty Condensate Pump**: Some AC models use electric pump to drain water upward to roof. Pump failure stops drainage despite gravity-powered line.

**Blocked Return Air**: Dirty filter or obstructed return vent causes coil temperature to drop excessively. Evaporator overproduces condensate beyond drain capacity.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Locate the drain line**: Follow indoor unit downward. Plastic tube (typically ½-inch) exits toward window or drain point. Check if it's kinked or blocked.

2. **Test drain flow**: Fill a cup of water and slowly pour into drain line outlet at outdoor end. Water should flow freely. If blocked, water backs up or drips from joint.

3. **Flush drain line with water**: Use garden hose to gently flush drain line from outdoor end. Push water toward indoor unit. May dislodge algae/debris. Repeat if murky water comes out.

4. **Check for ice**: Look at indoor unit evaporator coil (visible through intake vent). Any white frost or ice indicates freeze problem. This will cause water backup when thawing.

5. **Verify unit slope**: Indoor unit must tilt slightly toward drain outlet. Use spirit level—should drop ¼-inch over 2-feet length. If level or tilted wrong way, water cannot drain.

6. **Inspect drain pan**: If accessible, check plastic pan under coil. Look for visible cracks, separation, or corrosion holes.

7. **Clean intake filter**: Severely clogged filter increases coil icing and condensate production. Replace filter.

8. **Trace water path**: Note where water exits indoor unit. Should be from designated drain outlet only, not from gaps, seams, or joints.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard**: Water leaking toward wall cavity can reach electrical wiring or socket. Causes short circuit or electrocution risk. Do not touch wet wiring.

**Mold Growth**: Damp areas breed mold quickly in warm climate. Mold spores trigger respiratory issues. Ensure area dries completely after fixing leak.

**Structural Damage**: Prolonged water leak causes ceiling collapse, wall rot, and foundation damage. Act quickly.

**Slipping Hazard**: Water on floor is slipping hazard, especially on tile.

## When To Call A Technician
- Drain line consistently blocked despite flushing
- Water leaking from indoor unit housing seams (internal crack)
- Unit tilted wrong way and needs repositioning
- Condensate pump installed (electric drainage) not working
- Evaporator coil continuously icing
- Water leaking into wall cavity (structural risk)
- Drain pan corrosion/damage
- Unable to locate drain outlet

## Preventive Maintenance
- Flush drain line quarterly with water
- Replace air filter every 2-3 weeks during monsoon
- Ensure drain line slope toward outlet (minimum ¼-inch per 10 feet)
- Keep indoor unit level (check annually)
- Apply algae-inhibiting tablets to drain line (monthly during humid months)
- Ensure drain outlet is not submerged in water (install elevated outlet if needed)
- Use dehumidifier in adjacent room if AC moisture production is excessive
