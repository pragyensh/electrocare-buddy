# AC Weak Cooling

## Overview
Air conditioning unit blows cool air, but cooling capacity is insufficient to bring room temperature down to set point. Unit runs constantly but fails to achieve comfort. Common in 2-3 year old units or after refrigerant loss.

## Symptoms
- Room cools from 35°C to only 30°C (not reaching target 22°C)
- AC must run continuously, never cycles off
- Weak breeze from vents despite fan on high speed
- Electricity bills spike despite weaker performance
- Outdoor unit compressor extremely hot, almost untouchable
- Frost/ice visible inside indoor unit
- Condensation water output reduced

## Possible Causes
**Partial Refrigerant Leak**: Small leak allows unit to function but at reduced efficiency. Manifests as gradual performance degradation over weeks.

**Low Refrigerant Charge**: Installation undercharge or accumulated leak. Evaporator coil pressure too low, reducing cooling effect.

**Undersized AC for Room**: 1-ton AC cannot cool 300+ sqft room adequately. Check nameplate capacity versus room size.

**Dirty Condenser or Evaporator**: Dust coating on coils reduces heat transfer. Condenser cannot reject heat from compressed refrigerant. Evaporator cannot absorb heat from room air.

**Weak Outdoor Fan**: Fan motor wearing out, blade damaged, or bearings worn. Reduced airflow over condenser reduces heat rejection.

**Expansion Valve Malfunction**: Valve doesn't meter refrigerant correctly. Too little liquid reaches evaporator coil.

**Faulty Compressor**: Worn piston rings, leaking internal valves, or bearing wear reduces compression ratio. Compressor runs but doesn't pressurize effectively.

**Return Air Recirculation**: Hot exhaust from outdoor unit pulled back into indoor unit intake (poor placement). Reduces temperature differential.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Measure room cooling rate**: Check room temperature every 15 minutes. Plot on paper. Weak cooling shows linear temp drop <1°C per 15 minutes.

2. **Verify AC capacity**: Check indoor unit nameplate. Typical: 1 ton (3.5 kW), 1.5 ton (5.2 kW), 2 ton (7 kW). Match against room size. 1 ton cools ~120-150 sqft.

3. **Clean/replace filter**: Severely clogged filter can reduce capacity by 20-30%. Replace with manufacturer-approved filter.

4. **Check intake positioning**: Ensure outdoor unit intake is 1m away from walls and not in direct sun. Place shade cloth if outdoor temperature exceeds 45°C.

5. **Listen to compressor**: Should have steady mechanical vibration/hum. Intermittent cycling suggests low charge or thermostat cycling.

6. **Feel discharge tube temperature**: Outdoor compressor discharge tube should be extremely hot (>60°C if touchable). If warm/cold, charge is likely low.

7. **Inspect for frost**: Look at indoor unit evaporator coil through intake vent. Any frost/ice indicates imminent coil freeze.

8. **Check thermostat setting**: Verify it's not accidentally set to 30°C or higher. Lower setting by 2-3°C and observe response within 5 minutes.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard**: High-voltage capacitors store charge even when unplugged. Do not open the outdoor electrical panel.

**Refrigerant**: Even at partial charge, refrigerant is under high pressure. Rupturing a line causes instant frostbite and vapor release.

**Heat Stress**: Weak AC in hot climate can lead to heat exhaustion. Don't rely on malfunctioning AC during extreme heat events—seek alternative cooling.

## When To Call A Technician
- Room cools at <1°C per 20 minutes
- Compressor extremely hot (nearly untouchable)
- Frost building on indoor unit
- Unit cycling off/on every 2-3 minutes
- Suspected refrigerant leak
- Outdoor fan not running
- Burnt smell from unit
- Unit never reaches set temperature despite 2+ hours running

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean filters monthly during cooling season
- Professional coil cleaning every 4 months
- Ensure outdoor unit placement is shaded and ventilated
- Avoid placing AC in high-sun walls (west/south facing)
- Check refrigerant charge annually
- Verify voltage stability; use 5kVA stabilizer minimum for 1.5-ton AC
- Cover outdoor unit during monsoon to prevent dust/moisture ingress
