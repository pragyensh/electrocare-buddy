# AC Outdoor Unit Not Running

## Overview
Outdoor compressor and fan not operating despite indoor unit powered and set to cooling mode. May cause partial cooling (evaporator coil acts as filter) or no cooling at all. Caused by electrical disconnection, contactor failure, capacitor malfunction, or compressor mechanical lock.

## Symptoms
- Indoor unit powers on and displays temperature
- Indoor fan runs normally
- Compressor does not hum or vibrate
- Outdoor fan completely still
- No cooling effect despite running AC
- No response to temperature set changes
- Clicking sound from outdoor unit (repeated) then silence
- Breaker for outdoor unit trips repeatedly

## Possible Causes
**Electrical Disconnection**: Main outdoor contactor (magnetic switch) not engaged. Could be loose terminal, blown fuse, or tripped breaker.

**Capacitor Failure**: Capacitor provides initial current surge to start compressor and motor. Faulty capacitor prevents starting. Common in 3-4 year old units.

**Contactor Stuck/Failed**: Magnetic contactor (relay) receives 24V signal from indoor thermostat but doesn't physically engage. Welded or stuck contacts.

**Thermal Overload Trip**: Compressor overheated and safety switch tripped. Prevents damage but blocks operation until cooled.

**Refrigerant Pressure Issue**: High/low pressure switch prevents compressor startup if pressures out of range. Safety feature to avoid damage.

**Compressor Mechanical Lock**: Internal mechanical jam prevents rotor from turning. Rare but occurs after long idle or mechanical damage.

**Blown Fuse in Outdoor Unit**: Indoor fuse or outdoor disconnect fuse blown, breaking power circuit.

**Low Voltage Supply**: Main supply voltage below 180V. Contactor requires minimum 200V to engage properly.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check power supply**: Is outdoor unit breaker ON? Look at electrical panel. If breaker is OFF or tripped, reset by turning OFF then back ON. If trips again immediately, electrical fault present—stop and call technician.

2. **Listen for clicking**: Turn on AC. Does outdoor unit make clicking sound? Single click then hum = normal start. Rapid clicking every few seconds = capacitor likely failed. Silence = no power reaching contactor.

3. **Feel compressor body**: Wait 30 seconds after starting AC. Touch compressor housing carefully. Should be cool initially, warming up after 2-3 minutes. If stone cold after 5 minutes, compressor may not be running.

4. **Verify thermostat**: Set temperature 5°C below room temperature. Wait 3 minutes. Turn thermostat OFF then back ON. Any change in outdoor unit behavior?

5. **Check voltage at outdoor unit**: Use multimeter to measure voltage at main outdoor disconnect (if accessible and safe). Should read 220-240V. Below 180V indicates supply issue.

6. **Look for loose wires**: Visually inspect outdoor unit electrical connections (if safe and without opening panel). Any visibly loose or disconnected terminal?

7. **Test reset on contactor**: Some units have reset button on contactor. Press gently if visible. Wait 30 seconds. Power on AC again.

8. **Isolate the indoor unit**: Turn off indoor unit at breaker for 5 minutes. Turn back on. Does outdoor unit engage now? Sometimes indoor unit power surge causes outdoor unit to shut down for protection.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard**: Main outdoor circuit runs at 230V. Potentially lethal. Do not attempt to open electrical panel or touch exposed terminals without proper training.

**High Pressure**: Outdoor compressor operates at 20-25 bar pressure. Rupture causes high-speed refrigerant/oil spray and injury.

**Thermal Overload**: Compressor gets hot (60°C+). Burns from touching during/after operation. Wait 30 minutes after shutdown.

**Refrigerant**: Some compressor overheating is caused by refrigerant issues. Do not attempt recharge without proper training.

## When To Call A Technician
- Electrical breaker trips repeatedly when AC powered on
- Voltage supply confirmed below 200V
- Outdoor unit makes rapid clicking but doesn't start
- Compressor very hot (impossible to touch) but not running
- Thermal overload trips and resets cycle
- No voltage at outdoor disconnect despite indoor unit on
- Strange electrical smell from outdoor unit
- Compressor doesn't hum after 3 minutes of AC operation

## Preventive Maintenance
- Install voltage stabilizer (minimum 5 kVA for 1.5-ton AC) to protect electrical components
- Annual inspection of outdoor electrical connections
- Test starting behavior monthly (listen for contactor engagement)
- Ensure outdoor unit is not exposed to water during monsoon (cover intake during rain)
- Keep outdoor unit clear of debris (leaves, plastic bags that block fan)
- Do not operate AC during power fluctuations or low-voltage periods
- Have capacitor inspected/replaced every 3-4 years as preventive measure
