# AC Compressor Issue

## Overview
Compressor not starting, running weakly, making unusual noise, or shutting down prematurely. Compressor is the heart of AC system—when it fails, cooling stops. Issues range from electrical (won't start) to mechanical (worn internals) to thermal (overheating).

## Symptoms
- Compressor humming but not starting (locked rotor)
- Compressor starts then shuts down after seconds
- Very hot compressor that shuts down automatically
- Oil leaking from compressor
- Loud grinding, knocking, or clunking from compressor
- Compressor vibrating excessively
- Compressor weak (room cooling very slowly)
- Compressor trips thermal protection repeatedly

## Possible Causes
**Locked Rotor**: Compressor rotor stuck due to mechanical jam, rust, or sludge buildup from old/degraded oil. Most common after long idle.

**Capacitor Failure**: Start capacitor doesn't provide initial boost to overcome rotor inertia. Rotor hums but cannot start.

**Low Oil Level**: Compressor lubrication insufficient. Pistons/valves wear quickly, friction increases, rotor locks or bearing seizes.

**Worn Pistons/Valves**: After years of operation, internal valve wear reduces compression. Compressor runs but at low efficiency.

**Internal Valve Leakage**: Discharge valve damaged. High-pressure gas leaks back into intake, reducing net compression.

**Bearing Wear**: Compressor bearings worn, creating excessive friction. Motor cannot overcome resistance. Bearing failure progresses to seizure.

**Refrigerant Sludge**: Moisture in refrigerant reacts with oil to form acidic sludge. Sludge coats pistons/valves, increasing friction.

**Overpressure Condition**: High-side pressure too high (blocked condenser). Causes thermal overload shutdown.

**Underpressure Condition**: Low-side pressure too low (refrigerant leak). Causes thermal overload shutdown.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Monitor thermal cycling**: Run AC. Does compressor shut down after 10-30 minutes despite cool room? Write down times. Thermal protection is likely active. Indicates electrical or mechanical issue.

2. **Listen to startup sound**: When AC first turns on, listen for 5 seconds. Sound progression: click (contactor engagement) → hum → mechanical vibration. No hum after click = locked rotor. Hum then immediate click = overpressure.

3. **Feel compressor vibration**: After 2-3 minutes running, compressor should vibrate steadily. No vibration = not actually running. Excessive vibration = mechanical wear.

4. **Check for leaks**: Inspect around compressor for oil. Dark oil or oil/refrigerant mix indicates internal seal failure.

5. **Observe pressure gauges**: If accessible on service ports, compare high/low-side pressures to nameplate specs. Outside range indicates issue.

6. **Measure temperature rise**: Room temperature should drop 1°C per 15-20 minutes. Slower rate or no drop = weak compression.

7. **Try manual restart**: If compressor shuts down, wait 5 minutes. Manually turn AC back on. Does it restart? Multiple restart failures = thermal protection working as designed (electrical/mechanical issue present).

8. **Check for liquid flooding**: Very low outdoor unit temperature = liquid refrigerant reaching compressor (should be vapor only). This causes compressor damage.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: High-voltage contactor and capacitor circuits present. Do not attempt internal repair.

**Heat Hazard**: Running compressor reaches 60-80°C. Burns easily. Wait minimum 30 minutes after shutdown before touching.

**Pressure**: Compressor contains 20+ bar pressure internally. Rupture causes high-speed oil/refrigerant spray and explosion risk.

**Refrigerant**: Any compressor repair requires proper EPA-certified technician. Refrigerant release is environmental hazard and health risk.

## When To Call A Technician
- Locked rotor (hums but doesn't start)
- Oil visible leaking from compressor
- Grinding or knocking noise from compressor
- Compressor extremely hot (>70°C) with thermal shutdown
- Pressure readings outside specification
- Thermal protection tripping despite normal temperatures
- Weak cooling after years of normal operation
- Compressor requires replacement

## Preventive Maintenance
- Change/inspect compressor oil every 2-3 years
- Maintain proper refrigerant charge (not overcharged/undercharged)
- Keep condenser clean to prevent overpressure
- Use dehumidifier to prevent moisture in refrigerant lines
- Install filter-drier in refrigerant line to trap sludge
- Allow AC to sit for 5 minutes between on/off cycles
- Use voltage stabilizer to prevent electrical surges to compressor
- Have annual professional inspection of compressor condition
- Never run AC in reverse mode excessively (heating in winter)
