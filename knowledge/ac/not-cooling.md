# AC Not Cooling

## Overview
Air conditioning unit runs but fails to reduce room temperature. This is the most common AC complaint and can stem from refrigerant loss, compressor malfunction, airflow obstruction, or thermostat failure.

## Symptoms
- Warm air blowing from indoor unit despite unit running
- Unit cycles on and off without maintaining set temperature
- Reduced air pressure from vents
- Moisture condensation missing from drainage
- Unit running continuously without reaching target temperature
- Energy consumption higher than normal for the season

## Possible Causes
**Refrigerant Leak**: Most common cause. Refrigerant carries heat out of the room. Even small leaks reduce cooling capacity over weeks. Causes include corrosion in copper tubing, loose connections at compressor or condenser, or physical damage to outdoor unit.

**Dirty Condenser Coil**: Outdoor unit accumulates dust, pollen, and debris. Thick coating prevents heat rejection from compressed refrigerant. Common in coastal areas (salt spray) and high-pollution zones. Blocks airflow through fins.

**Evaporator Coil Freezing**: Indoor coil freezes when refrigerant temperature drops below dew point. Causes: low refrigerant charge, restricted airflow, thermostat sensor malfunction, or faulty expansion valve. Frozen coil stops heat transfer completely.

**Compressor Failure**: Compressor is the heart of AC. May not start due to capacitor failure, electrical contactor issue, or mechanical wear. If it's running but weak, internal valve leakage or worn pistons reduce compression ratio.

**Faulty Thermostat/Temperature Sensor**: Sensor misreads room temperature. Thermostat stuck in low setting or sensor dirty. Causes compressor to cycle incorrectly.

**Low Voltage Supply**: AC needs stable 230V±10%. Low voltage (under 200V) prevents compressor from starting or reduces its efficiency dramatically. Check main supply and wiring.

**Airflow Blockage**: Dirty air filters restrict indoor air circulation. Blocked return air vent reduces evaporator heat absorption. Prevents saturation of indoor air with cooled air.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check thermostat**: Set temperature 5°C below current room temperature. Wait 2 minutes. If indoor unit fan speeds up but compressor doesn't start, issue is electrical/compressor-related.

2. **Inspect air filter**: Open indoor unit's front panel. Check if white filter is gray/black with dust. Filthy filter blocks 40-60% of airflow. Replace if necessary.

3. **Check return air vent**: Ensure return air is not blocked by furniture, curtains, or closed doors. Return air must flow freely back to indoor unit.

4. **Feel compressor tube**: Outdoor unit compressor gets hot when running. Touch discharge tube (thick tube near compressor). Should be too hot to hold hand on. If cold, compressor may not be running or is severely undercharged.

5. **Listen for sounds**: Compressor should hum/vibrate when active. Clicking before humming is normal (contactor engaging). Silence suggests electrical failure. Loud grinding is mechanical damage.

6. **Check outdoor unit**: Is outdoor fan running? If not, issue is electrical or fan motor failure. Clean visible dirt/leaves around intake.

7. **Inspect for water**: Under indoor unit should have water dripping into drainage line. No water suggests low refrigerant or frozen coil.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard**: AC compressor and contactor run at 230V. Never touch internal electrical components. Do not open condenser or evaporator without isolation.

**Refrigerant Hazard**: R32 refrigerant is mildly flammable. High-pressure hoses can cause frostbite if ruptured. Never attempt to refill refrigerant yourself—requires EPA-licensed technician.

**Mechanical Hazard**: Compressor and outdoor fan create entanglement risk. Keep hands/clothing away from outdoor unit intake/discharge.

## When To Call A Technician
- Compressor not starting despite power supply
- Refrigerant leak confirmed (oily spots, hissing sound)
- Evaporator coil frozen
- Capacitor suspected (clicking but no compressor start)
- Compressor making grinding/knocking sounds
- Temperature drop <3°C over 30 minutes of running
- Any electrical concerns or burnt smell

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean intake filter every 15 days during cooling season
- Professional coil cleaning every 6 months
- Check outdoor unit for debris weekly
- Ensure drain line is clear (pour water through indoor unit drain)
- Install stabilizer if voltage fluctuates below 200V
- Get annual professional inspection before peak summer
