# AC Remote Not Working

## Overview
AC remote control fails to operate unit or has intermittent response. May be caused by battery depletion, IR sensor malfunction, faulty remote circuit, or receiver issue. Prevents convenient AC control; unit may still be operated manually.

## Symptoms
- Remote buttons do not trigger AC response
- Intermittent response (works sometimes)
- LED indicator on remote does not light
- Unit responds to manual button panel but not remote
- Reduced remote range (only works 1-2 meters away)
- Remote works for some functions but not others
- AC displays "remote" indicator but doesn't respond

## Possible Causes
**Dead Batteries**: Most common cause (60% of cases). Remote runs on two AAA or AA batteries. Batteries deplete after 6-12 months depending on usage.

**Weak Battery Contact**: Batteries not seated properly or battery terminals corroded. Reduces power transmission to IR LED.

**Faulty IR LED on Remote**: LED chip damaged or desolderd from circuit board. No infrared signal emitted.

**Dirty IR Receiver Lens**: Dust accumulation on indoor unit IR receiver reduces signal reception. Especially common in coastal areas.

**IR Receiver Circuit Failure**: Receiver PCB in indoor unit malfunctions. Cannot decode incoming IR signal.

**Remote Button Contact Failure**: Rubber button pad worn out or conductive coating erased. Button pressed but no circuit completion.

**RF Interference**: Other devices (LED lights, phone chargers, radio) emit RF signals that jam IR communication.

**Incorrect Remote Model**: Wrong remote paired with AC unit. Some ACs have specific remote codes.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Replace batteries**: Remove battery cover at bottom of remote. Replace both batteries with fresh alkaline AAA or AA (as specified in remote manual). Ensure polarity is correct (+/- orientation).

2. **Test battery type**: Verify correct battery type in remote manual. Do not mix old and new batteries. All batteries should be same brand and age.

3. **Check LED emission**: In dark room, point remote at phone camera lens (not viewfinder). Press any button. If LED is working, you'll see red/pink flash on camera screen. No flash = LED failure.

4. **Clean IR receiver**: Locate IR receiver window on indoor unit (small black lens, usually at top of unit). Gently wipe with dry microfiber cloth. Remove dust.

5. **Reduce RF interference**: Turn off nearby LED lamps or phone chargers. Wireless devices operating on 2.4 GHz can interfere. Move away and test.

6. **Test at different angle**: Point remote directly at receiver at 0-30 degree angle. IR signals don't reflect well at steep angles. Try perpendicular approach.

7. **Test manual panel**: Press buttons on manual control panel built into indoor unit. If manual works, issue is remote/receiver, not compressor or electrical.

8. **Try universal remote**: Borrow universal AC remote from neighbor or rent from electronics store. If universal works, your remote is faulty.

9. **Check for physical damage**: Inspect remote for cracks, water damage, or loose internals. Any liquid ingress causes circuit failure.

## Safety Warnings
**Battery Hazard**: Alkaline batteries can leak if depleted or mixed old/new. Corrosive battery acid damages remote circuit. Replace entire set with fresh batteries when depleted.

**IR LED Safety**: Do not look directly into IR LED when testing. IR is invisible but can damage eye at close range (over 1 meter generally safe).

**Electrical**: Do not open indoor unit receiver module. High-voltage components inside.

## When To Call A Technician
- LED test confirms IR LED not emitting (remote chip failure)
- Remote physically damaged or water-damaged
- After battery replacement, remote still non-functional
- IR receiver window has internal condensation or visible damage
- Unit responds to manual panel but not remote (receiver module likely failed)
- Multiple remotes don't work with same unit (receiver issue)
- Remote needs reprogramming with unit

## Preventive Maintenance
- Replace remote batteries every 8-12 months proactively
- Avoid exposing remote to moisture, heat, or direct sunlight
- Do not drop or apply excessive pressure on remote buttons
- Keep remote away from small children and pets
- Store remote in cool, dry place
- Clean IR receiver lens quarterly with dry cloth
- Avoid pointing remote at reflective surfaces (mirror bounces can cause misfire)
- Keep remote away from RF sources (microwave, wireless router, cordless phones)
