# AC High Electricity Bill

## Overview
AC energy consumption exceeds expectations or previous bills. Monthly consumption increased despite same usage pattern. Caused by reduced efficiency, compressor overwork, refrigerant loss, or electrical fault. Diagnosing source helps optimize costs.

## Symptoms
- Electricity bill 30-50% higher than last season
- AC runs longer to reach target temperature
- Unit never cycles off (always compressor running)
- Energy meter running faster when AC on
- Weak cooling despite high runtime
- Outdoor compressor extremely hot
- Room temperature fluctuates (unit cycling rapidly)
- High-side refrigerant pressure reading (if accessible)

## Possible Causes
**Low Refrigerant Charge**: Compressor works harder to reduce pressure, consuming more energy for same cooling effect.

**Dirty Condenser Coil**: Restricted airflow forces compressor to run longer. High-side pressure increases, compressor works harder.

**Dirty Evaporator Coil**: Reduced heat absorption from room air. Unit must run longer to cool room.

**Undersized AC**: 1-ton AC running 24/7 to cool large room. Insufficient capacity for space.

**Thermostat Set Too Low**: Setting to 16°C instead of 22°C doubles runtime. Each degree below 22°C increases energy by ~6%.

**Faulty Thermostat**: Thermostat doesn't register target temp reached. AC cycles continuously even when room is cool.

**Compressor Wear**: Worn internal components (pistons, valves) reduce efficiency. More energy wasted as heat.

**Electrical Fault**: Partially shorted winding in motor draws excess current.

**Poor Insulation**: Room not properly insulated. Heat gain exceeds AC cooling capacity.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Compare bills**: Look at previous 3 months of bills before/after cooling season start. Note consumption per day. Doubling = significant issue.

2. **Measure runtime**: For 1 hour, note AC on/off cycles. Should cycle OFF at least 10-20 minutes per hour during stable temperature. If always on, issue exists.

3. **Optimize thermostat**: Set to 22-24°C (common comfort range). Reduce to 20°C only if necessary. Each degree lower = ~6% higher bill.

4. **Check room insulation**: Ensure room is well-sealed. Close gaps around windows/doors. Poor insulation forces AC to work harder.

5. **Clean filters**: Clogged filter forces unit to work 20% harder. Replace filter. Monitor bill next month.

6. **Clean outdoor unit**: Pressure-wash outdoor condenser coil (or spray with garden hose). Dirt coating reduces efficiency significantly.

7. **Check temperature differential**: Room should cool from 35°C to 22°C in 30-45 minutes. Longer time = reduced efficiency.

8. **Measure compressor runtime**: If compressor runs 55+ minutes per hour, refrigerant charge or cooling efficiency is likely poor.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical**: Do not attempt to inspect internal compressor windings. High-voltage motor present.

**Refrigerant Pressure**: Never attempt to release or recharge refrigerant. High-pressure hazard.

## When To Call A Technician
- Bill doubled despite same usage pattern
- Compressor running continuously (no cycling)
- Weak cooling despite high runtime
- Measured high-side pressure exceeds specification (requires gauge)
- Compressor bearing sounds abnormal (grinding)
- Room never cools below 26°C (undersized unit or major efficiency loss)
- Thermostat not responding to setting changes

## Preventive Maintenance
- Replace filter every 20 days during cooling season
- Professional condenser cleaning every 3-4 months
- Evaporator coil inspection/cleaning annually
- Thermostat calibration check annually
- Refrigerant charge verification annually
- Door/window weatherstripping replacement every 2 years
- Install window films/shades on sunny exposures (reduce solar heat gain by 30%)
- Use programmable thermostat (automatic temp reduction during night/absence)
- Run AC only during night hours if possible (cooler outdoor temp = lower compressor load)
- Maintain 22-24°C setpoint during occupied hours
