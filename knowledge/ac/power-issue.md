# AC Power Issue

## Overview
AC unit fails to power on or powers on intermittently. Main breaker may trip when AC starts. Display shows no lights or intermittent display. Caused by electrical supply problems, wiring faults, tripped breaker, or internal electrical component failure.

## Symptoms
- AC does not respond when powered on
- Display blank (no LED lights)
- Power comes on then immediately goes off
- Main electrical breaker trips when AC starts
- Intermittent power (works sometimes, not others)
- AC powers on but indoor/outdoor unit non-responsive
- Burning smell from electrical connections
- Flickering of other lights when AC starts

## Possible Causes
**Tripped Breaker**: Most common. Excessive current draw trips breaker for safety. Occurs at startup (inrush current).

**Low Voltage Supply**: Main supply 180V or below. Cannot sustain AC operation. Compressor won't start, thermal protection may engage.

**Faulty Wiring/Loose Connection**: Corrosion, loose terminal, or damaged wire increases resistance. Voltage drop prevents normal operation.

**Short Circuit**: Damaged wiring insulation causes phase-to-phase or phase-to-ground short. Creates large current, trips breaker immediately.

**Faulty Indoor PCB**: Power supply circuit on main board damaged. Cannot regulate power to unit.

**Faulty Outdoor Contactor**: Contactor draws excessive current at engagement, trips breaker.

**Capacitor Short**: Start capacitor or run capacitor internally shorted, draws excessive inrush current.

**Installation Error**: AC connected to wrong voltage (380V three-phase instead of 230V single-phase). Catastrophic failure.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check main breaker**: Locate electrical panel. Is AC breaker in OFF position? Check for trips—breaker switch should be in middle or fully OFF if tripped. Reset by turning to OFF then fully ON. Wait 30 seconds. Try AC again. If trips immediately, electrical fault present.

2. **Test other appliances**: Try large appliance (refrigerator, microwave) on same circuit. If they work fine, issue is AC-specific. If others also fail, main supply problem or overloaded circuit.

3. **Measure supply voltage**: Using multimeter, measure voltage at wall socket where AC should connect. Reading should be 220-240V. Below 200V indicates low voltage situation. If below 180V, contact electricity board.

4. **Check connection terminals**: If safe and accessible, look at AC terminal block where wires enter. Are terminals loose? Any corrosion or burn marks? Do not touch if electricity is on.

5. **Look for burn marks**: Inspect power cord and indoor unit power inlet. Any blackening, melting, or burn marks indicate short circuit or arcing.

6. **Try different outlet**: If AC is on removable outlet, try plugging into different room outlet on different breaker. Does it work? If yes, original outlet/circuit has issue.

7. **Isolate AC circuit**: Turn off all other devices on AC circuit. Try AC alone. If it works, original circuit was overloaded. Add to different circuit if possible.

8. **Delay startup**: If breaker trips on startup, wait 5 minutes between attempts. Inrush current may be time-sensitive. Breaker may reset faster if cooled.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrocution**: Main electrical panel contains 230V. Lethal voltage. Do not open panel or touch terminals unless trained and de-energized.

**Fire Hazard**: Loose connections or shorts create extreme heat. Fire risk in walls/equipment. Turn off immediately if burning smell detected.

**Magnetic Hazard**: Never reset breaker more than 3 times if it keeps tripping. Repeated trips indicate serious fault. Continued operation risks fire/injury.

## When To Call A Technician
- Breaker trips immediately after reset (do not retry—call electrician)
- Supply voltage confirmed below 200V (contact electricity board or electrician)
- Burning smell from power connections
- AC was recently installed and never powered successfully
- Visible burn/char marks inside or on AC unit
- Tripped breaker while AC was running normally (suggests internal electrical component failure)
- Flickering lights in entire house when AC starts (indicates unstable supply)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Install voltage stabilizer (minimum 5 kVA for 1.5-ton AC)
- Use dedicated breaker for AC (not shared with other devices)
- Install surge protector between supply and AC
- Have annual electrical inspection by certified electrician
- Do not use extension cords for AC (direct wall connection recommended)
- Ensure all connections are tight (check annually)
- Avoid operating AC during thunderstorms (power surge risk)
- Keep indoor unit away from damp areas (prevents short circuits)
- Use differential protection (RCCB) in addition to breaker for safety
