# Geyser Not Heating

## Overview
Geyser powered on but water doesn't heat. Water comes out cold or lukewarm despite geyser running. Caused by failed heating element, damaged thermostat, tripped thermal cutout, or control circuit failure.

## Symptoms
- Water remains cold after waiting 10+ minutes
- Geyser powered but no heating
- Heating indicator light off
- Heating element not glowing/red
- Thermostat not responding
- Water temperature inconsistent
- Thermal cutout tripped (red button)

## Possible Causes
**Failed Heating Element": Electric resistance wire burned out. Cannot generate heat.

**Faulty Thermostat": Temperature sensor doesn't detect desired temperature. Never activates heating.

**Tripped Thermal Cutout": Safety switch engages if temperature exceeds limit. Cuts power to heating element.

**Thermostat Bulb Leak": Mercury-based thermostat bulb cracked. No temperature sensing possible.

**Loose Heating Element Connection": Electrical connection to element loose. No current flows to element.

**Failed Control Relay": Relay that switches heating element on/off doesn't engage.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check power": Is geyser plugged in? Does display show power (if it has one)? No display light = power issue.

2. **Check breaker": Has home breaker for geyser tripped? Look at electrical panel. Toggle OFF then ON if tripped.

3. **Locate thermal cutout": Usually red button on geyser body or thermostat. Is button popped out (red showing)? If yes, press it (gently) to reset. Wait 10 minutes. Try heating again.

4. **Feel heating element": Is heating element warm after 5 minutes of operation? No heat = element failed or power not reaching it.

5. **Check thermostat setting": Is temperature dial set appropriately (not to minimum)? Turn dial slightly. Does heating activate?

6. **Listen for relay click": When pressing thermostat button/turning dial, listen for click sound (relay engaging). No click = relay or control circuit failed.

7. **Check water inlet": Is water flowing into geyser? No water = heating element damage (dry running damaged it).

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical": Heating element operates at 230V AC. Do not touch while powered.

**Thermal Hazard": If heating suddenly resumes after cutout reset, water can be scalding. Test temperature before full immersion.

## When To Call A Technician
- Heating element confirmed failed (no continuity test shows resistance)
- Thermostat not responding to adjustments
- Thermal cutout resets but immediately trips again (overheating risk)
- Water inlet flow issue suspected

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Heating element replacement: ₹1500–₹3000
- Thermostat replacement: ₹800–₹1500
- Thermal cutout reset: ₹300–₹600 (if mechanical stuck)
- Control relay replacement: ₹600–₹1200
- Complete thermostat with bulb: ₹1200–₹2500

## Preventive Maintenance
- Test heating monthly (ensure it reaches desired temperature)
- Check thermal cutout quarterly (press gently to verify functioning)
- Inspect connections yearly
- Descale heating element annually (prevents mineral damage)
- Use voltage stabilizer (protects element from surges)
