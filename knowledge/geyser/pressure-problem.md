# Geyser Pressure Problem

## Overview
Water pressure from geyser outlet is weak or inconsistent. Water trickles instead of flowing. Affects usability for shower. Caused by clogged outlet, failing relief valve, scale in pipes, or inlet valve restriction.

## Symptoms
- Very weak water flow from outlet
- Pressure fluctuates (weak then normal)
- Water trickles from shower
- Outlet aerator blocked
- Pressure gauge shows low reading (if present)
- Pressure reduces over time after heating
- Cold water pressure normal; hot water pressure low

## Possible Causes
**Clogged Outlet Aerator": Inlet screen gets mineral buildup. Blocks flow.

**Scale in Outlet Pipes": Mineral deposits line outlet pipes. Restrict flow.

**Relief Valve Stuck Partially Open": Safety valve releases pressure even when not needed.

**Inlet Stop Valve Partially Closed": Cold water inlet valve not fully open.

**Pressure Regulator Issue": If installed, regulator may be set too low or malfunctioning.

**Cracked/Blocked Outlet Line": Internal damage restricts flow.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check hot vs cold**: Is cold water pressure normal? If cold pressure weak too, building water supply issue (not geyser).

2. **Check other outlets": Does other hot water outlet in house have pressure? If yes, outlet line from geyser to this location clogged.

3. **Clean aerator": Locate outlet aerator (screen at tap). Unscrew. Soak in vinegar 1 hour to dissolve mineral buildup. Scrub with old toothbrush. Rinse thoroughly. Reinstall.

4. **Check inlet valve": At geyser inlet, locate stop valve (manual on/off). Is it fully open? Turn counterclockwise to maximum.

5. **Test relief valve": Locate relief valve (usually small handle on tank). Lift handle to release pressure. Listen for hissing. If no hissing, pressure low (normal).

6. **Feel pressure gauge": If geyser has pressure gauge, note reading. Should be 2-3 bar (display shows number). Low reading = pressure issue.

7. **Flush outlet": Some geysers have drain/flush valve. Open slightly to flush outlet line. Any discolored water? If yes, scale or sediment present. Close valve after flushing.

## Safety Warnings
**Pressure Release": When lifting relief valve, pressurized hot water releases. Wear gloves/safety glasses.

**Scalding Water": Pressure problem may cause sudden pressure surge when fixed. Water can be scalding. Test temperature gradually.

## When To Call A Technician
- Cold water pressure also low (plumbing issue)
- Relief valve stuck open (replacement needed)
- Internal pipes suspected cracked
- Pressure regulator needs adjustment

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Aerator cleaning: ₹300–₹600
- Outlet line descaling: ₹1500–₹3000
- Inlet valve repair/replacement: ₹800–₹1500
- Relief valve replacement: ₹800–₹1500
- Pressure regulator adjustment: ₹600–₹1200

## Preventive Maintenance
- Clean aerator monthly (prevent blockage)
- Flush outlet annually
- Check inlet valve quarterly (ensure fully open)
- Descale pipes annually (prevents buildup)
- Test relief valve quarterly
