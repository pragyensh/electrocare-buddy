# Geyser Strange Noise

## Overview
Unusual sounds coming from geyser during operation. Crackling, popping, hissing, or rumbling. Indicates mineral buildup, boiling, or component movement. Noise increases over time if mineral scale accumulates.

## Symptoms
- Popping or crackling noise during heating
- Rumbling sound from tank
- Hissing from relief valve
- Loud banging when heating stops
- Gurgling noise from outlet
- Vibrating sounds
- High-pitched squealing noise

## Possible Causes
**Mineral Scale Buildup": Scale inside tank creates deposits. Water boiling around scale produces crackling/popping.

**Boiling Water": Water overheating (thermostat failed). Water rapidly boils, creating noise.

**Relief Valve Operation": Relief valve releasing excess pressure. Normal but may be noisier if needs maintenance.

**Thermal Expansion": Metal tank expands when heating. Banging as metal hits frame or support.

**Loose Tank Mounting": Tank not secured properly. Vibrates during heating.

**Air in Pipes": Air pockets create gurgling. Normal but can be annoying.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Identify noise type": Crackling = scale buildup. Hissing = relief valve. Banging = thermal expansion/mounting. Gurgling = air in pipes.

2. **Listen during heating": When geyser heating, where loudest? Top = boiling. Bottom = scale on heating element. Side = thermal expansion.

3. **Check tank mounting": Look at geyser installation. Is it secured firmly to wall/floor? Any loose bolts or straps? Gentle shake—any movement?

4. **Flush relief valve": Locate relief valve (usually small handle on tank). Lift/pull handle slightly to let air/excess pressure out. May reduce hissing.

5. **Descale hint": If crackling/popping noise, mineral scale likely. This will eventually reduce efficiency. Consider descaling service.

6. **Check thermostat": Is setting appropriate (not too high)? Turn dial slightly lower. Does noise reduce? If yes, overheating suspected.

## Safety Warnings
**Relief Valve Spray": When flushing relief valve, hot water sprays out. Wear safety glasses/gloves. Be cautious.

**Overheating Risk**: Excessive boiling noise may indicate thermostat failure. If noise accompanied by very hot tank exterior, reduce setting or call technician.

## When To Call A Technician
- Relief valve continuously hissing (needs service)
- Tank has loose mounting (requires professional securing)
- Popping noise accompanied by visible leaks
- Banging severe and disruptive

## Preventive Maintenance
- Descale annually (prevents scale noise)
- Check mounting annually (ensure tight)
- Test relief valve quarterly
- Monitor thermostat (ensure not stuck on high)
- Flush air from pipes monthly
