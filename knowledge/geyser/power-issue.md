# Geyser Power Issue

## Overview
Geyser doesn't turn on or loses power intermittently. No lights, display, or heating function. Caused by tripped breaker, outlet failure, damaged cord, or internal electrical fault.

## Symptoms
- Geyser completely non-responsive
- No power lights/display visible
- Breaker keeps tripping
- Geyser powers on then off suddenly
- Plugged in but no power
- Intermittent power loss
- Burning smell near outlet/cord
- Cord visibly damaged

## Possible Causes
**Tripped Circuit Breaker": Geyser draws excessive current. Breaker trips as safety measure.

**Dead Outlet": Outlet itself not functioning (even though power present elsewhere).

**Damaged Power Cord": Cord frayed, cut, or electrically shorted.

**Internal Electrical Fault": Wiring inside geyser shorted. Causes breaker trip.

**Failed Power Switch": Switch inside geyser doesn't make electrical contact.

**Loose Internal Connection": Wiring connection loosened, causing intermittent power.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Check breaker": Look at home electrical panel. Is geyser breaker in OFF position or middle (tripped)? Toggle OFF then back ON. Does it stay on?

2. **Reset breaker three times max": If breaker trips repeatedly, issue is serious. Do not keep resetting. Call electrician.

3. **Test outlet": Plug lamp into outlet. Does lamp work? If lamp works but geyser doesn't, geyser has internal issue.

4. **Try different outlet": If possible, plug geyser into different outlet (different breaker). Does it work? If yes, original outlet/circuit has issue.

5. **Check power cord": Inspect entire length of cord. Any cuts, fraying, kinks, or burn marks? Damaged cord requires replacement.

6. **Feel cord for heat": When plugged in (before turning on), is cord warm? Should be cool. Warmth indicates internal short.

7. **Listen for humming": When turned on, should hear faint hum (heating element). No hum = no power reaching heating system.

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard": If cord damaged or burnt, do not use geyser. Electrocution/fire risk.

**Circuit Failure": Breaker tripping repeatedly indicates electrical fault. Do not ignore. Professional help needed.

## When To Call A Technician
- Breaker keeps tripping despite multiple resets (internal short likely)
- Power cord damaged (replacement needed)
- Outlet tested and confirmed dead (electrician may need to check circuit)
- Geyser has internal electrical failure (professional diagnosis)

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Outlet repair/replacement: ₹300–₹600
- Power cord replacement: ₹400–₹800
- Circuit breaker replacement: ₹800–₹1500
- Internal wiring repair: ₹1200–₹2500
- Electrician call-out: ₹300–₹500

## Preventive Maintenance
- Check power cord monthly for damage
- Use voltage stabilizer (protects from surges)
- Don't overload circuit (use dedicated breaker for geyser)
- Plug into proper outlet (same floor level ideally, not extension cord)
- Have annual electrical inspection
