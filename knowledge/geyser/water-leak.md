# Geyser Water Leak

## Overview
Water leaking from geyser body, connections, or outlets. May occur during heating or when idle. Risk of water damage and electrical hazard. Caused by corrosion holes, loose connections, damaged gaskets, or relief valve failure.

## Symptoms
- Water dripping from geyser bottom
- Water pooling under geyser
- Wet patches on geyser body
- Water leaking from connection joints
- Water coming from relief valve outlet
- Water dripping from pressure gauge
- Reduced water output
- Rusted areas on tank

## Possible Causes
**Tank Corrosion": Interior rust/corrosion creates small holes. Water seeps through.

**Loose Connection": Inlet/outlet pipe connection not tight. Water leaks at joint.

**Failed Relief Valve": Safety valve that releases excess pressure stuck open. Water drips constantly.

**Damaged Gasket": Rubber gasket at connection point deteriorated. Water seeps.

**Cracked Thermostat Well": Thermostat tube opening cracked. Water leaks around it.

**Pipe Fracture": Visible crack in supply/outlet pipes.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Locate leak source": Observe carefully during heating. Where does water drip from? Connection, bottom, side, relief valve?

2. **Check connections": At inlet and outlet pipes, connections should be tight. Hand-tighten gently. Do not over-tighten (may crack).

3. **Inspect relief valve": Locate relief valve (usually small opening with pipe). Is water dripping steadily? Dripping = valve stuck open.

4. **Check thermostat": Look at thermostat insertion point. Any water pooling around it? Wetness = gasket failure or tube crack.

5. **Inspect tank for rust": Look at tank body. Any visible rust, pitting, or corrosion holes? Rust color (reddish/brown) indicates internal corrosion.

6. **Tighten inlet valve": If water leaks from inlet connection, gently tighten inlet stop valve (may have nut you can turn with wrench).

7. **Wipe and dry": Clean area. Dry completely. Observe for 30 minutes. Where does leak reappear?

## Safety Warnings
**Electrical Hazard": Water leaking toward electrical outlet = electrocution risk. Address immediately.

**Water Damage": Prolonged leak damages walls, ceilings, structure.

**Scalding": Leaking water may be very hot. Do not touch bare skin.

## When To Call A Technician
- Leak from tank body (corrosion hole—requires replacement)
- Connection leak not fixed by tightening
- Relief valve stuck open (needs replacement)
- Crack in supply lines
- Water around thermostat area (requires service)

## Preventive Maintenance
- Check connections quarterly (tighten if loose)
- Inspect for rust spots annually
- Test relief valve annually (should have slight drip)
- Use water softener if hard water area (prevents mineral scale)
- Drain geyser annually to remove sediment
- Install pressure gauge (monitor for excessive pressure)
