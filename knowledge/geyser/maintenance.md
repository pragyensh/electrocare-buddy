# Geyser Maintenance

## Overview
Regular geyser care extends lifespan 10-15 years, improves efficiency, prevents emergency failures, and reduces repair costs 40-50%. Proper maintenance is critical for water heater reliability.

## Weekly Maintenance

**Inspect for Leaks": Quick visual check around geyser base and connections. Any water pooling? Early detection prevents damage.

**Feel Tank Temperature": Touch tank body (not direct contact with heating element area). Warm? Normal. Very hot (uncomfortable to hold)? Thermostat may need adjustment.

**Test Hot Water Flow": Turn on hot water tap. Any change from usual pressure/temperature? Consistency indicates good operation.

## Monthly Maintenance

**Check Thermostat Function": Adjust thermostat dial slightly. Does water temperature respond? If change takes >10 minutes or no response, thermostat issue.

**Test Relief Valve": Locate relief valve (usually small handle). Gently lift/pull handle. Should hear slight hissing. Water may drip from outlet. This is normal—indicates valve working.

**Inspect Connections**: Check inlet/outlet pipe connections. Any water weeping at joints? Tighten gently if loose.

**Wipe Exterior": Clean geyser exterior with dry cloth. Remove dust from vents. Prevents airflow obstruction.

## Quarterly Maintenance

**Deep Inspection": Look for rust spots, corrosion, or discoloration on tank body. Photograph if visible. Note for technician if worsening.

**Check Thermal Cutout": If geyser has red thermal reset button, verify it's not popped out. Button fully in = normal. Popped out = previous overheat (investigate cause).

**Inspect Power Cord": Check plug and cord for damage. Any fraying, burn marks, or exposed wires? Replace immediately if damaged.

**Flush Sediment": Some geysers have drain valve at bottom. Open partially to let sediment drain (collect in bucket). Close tightly after.

## Annual Maintenance (Professional Recommended)

**Full System Inspection": Technician checks:
- Heating element condition and resistance
- Thermostat accuracy
- Relief valve operation
- All electrical connections
- Tank structural integrity
- Pressure gauge accuracy (if present)

**Descaling Service": Professional descaling removes mineral deposits from heating element. Improves efficiency 15-20%.

**Pressure Gauge Calibration": If geyser has gauge, verify reading accuracy.

**Tank Drain and Flush": Complete tank drain. Remove accumulated sediment. Refill and test.

## Every 2-3 Years

**Anode Rod Replacement": Sacrificial rod in tank prevents corrosion. When corroded, replace. If not replaced, tank corrodes and fails.

**Relief Valve Service": Relief valve may need maintenance or replacement (5-10 year lifespan).

## When To Replace Components

**Heating Element**: 8-15 years (or if failed)
**Thermostat": 5-10 years
**Relief Valve": 5-10 years
**Anode Rod": Every 2-3 years
**Insulation**: 10-15 years (if degraded)
**Power Cord**: 5-10 years (or if damaged)

## Descaling Procedures (DIY or Professional)

**DIY Mild Descaling**: 
1. Turn off geyser
2. Drain tank completely via bottom drain valve
3. Pour white vinegar solution (1:1 vinegar to water) into tank via inlet
4. Let sit 1-2 hours
5. Open outlet tap to flush vinegar through heating element
6. Drain completely
7. Refill with fresh water
8. Drain once more
9. Close drain valve
10. Refill and restart geyser

**Professional Descaling**: 
- Uses commercial descaling solutions
- Circulates solution through heating element
- More thorough than DIY
- Recommended annually

## Efficiency Optimization

**Set Thermostat to 60-65°C**: Higher settings (>70°C) increase energy use without benefit. Standard 60-65°C sufficient for most uses.

**Insulate Outlet Pipes**: Wrap pipes between geyser and tap with foam. Prevents heat loss. Saves energy.

**Use Timers/Setbacks**: Program geyser to heat only during needed times (morning/evening). Reduces standby energy loss.

**Drain Sediment Monthly": Keeps heating element clean. Improves efficiency.

**Install Pressure Gauge**: Monitors system pressure. Alerts to issues early.

## Electrical Safety Maintenance

**Use Voltage Stabilizer": Install 2 kVA stabilizer. Protects heating element and thermostat from voltage surges.

**Dedicated Outlet": Use outlet exclusively for geyser (not shared with other high-power appliances).

**Breaker Inspection": Home breaker should be 16A minimum for geyser. Have electrician verify.

**Annual Electrical Check**: Ensure no loose connections, burnt wires, or damage.

## Water Quality Impact

**Hard Water Area**: Install water softener or descale more frequently (every 6 months vs annually).

**Chlorinated Water": Chlorine accelerates corrosion. Use filters to reduce chlorine.

**Acidic Water": Some areas have naturally acidic water. May accelerate anode corrosion. More frequent anode replacement needed.

## Emergency Procedures

**Relief Valve Continuously Leaking": Indicates excessive pressure. Check thermostat setting. If set correctly, valve needs replacement. Professional help.

**Tank Extremely Hot (Untouchable)": Thermostat failed, stuck on maximum heat. Turn off immediately. Do not use. Call technician.

**Water Leak From Tank": If crack suspected, tank near end of life. Plan replacement. Use geyser minimally until professional assessment.

**Electrical Smell": Unplug geyser. Do not restart. Professional inspection required.
