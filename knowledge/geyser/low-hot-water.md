# Geyser Low Hot Water

## Overview
Geyser heats water but output quantity is insufficient or temperature drops quickly. Takes longer than usual to fill tub or shower. Caused by undersized tank for demand, failing heating element, or high cold-water mixing.

## Symptoms
- Hot water runs out quickly
- Takes 15+ minutes to fill bath
- Temperature drops after initial flow
- Low hot water pressure
- Only partial tub gets hot water
- Multiple-user households affected more
- Heating takes longer than before

## Possible Causes
**Undersized Geyser": Tank too small for household demand.

**Weak Heating Element": Element outputs less heat. Takes longer to heat water.

**Mineral Scale on Element": Scale coating reduces efficiency. Less heat transferred to water.

**Cold Water Mix": Hot and cold water mixing at outlet. Reduces effective hot water.

**Thermostat Set Too Low": Temperature set to 50°C instead of 60-65°C. Perceived lower hot water availability.

**Insulation Loss": Tank insulation degraded. Water cools faster after heating stops.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Measure tank capacity": Look at geyser specs. Is capacity 15L, 25L, 50L? Household size? More than 3 people, need 50L+ typically.

2. **Check thermostat setting": Is dial set to maximum safe temperature (usually 65°C, marked on dial)? Too low setting = less hot water perception.

3. **Descale heating element": Mineral buildup reduces efficiency. Running descaling solution through system can help (requires maintenance valve access or professional service).

4. **Check outlet temperature": Use thermometer. Is outlet actually hot (>60°C) or lukewarm? Actual temperature vs perception.

5. **Check cold water inlet": Is cold water inlet valve fully closed? Partial opening adds cold water to outlet (mixing).

6. **Observe heating time": Time how long geyser takes to reach set temperature after draining. >60 minutes = weak element or heavy scale.

7. **Check usage pattern": Multiple people showering in succession? First person gets hot water; second person gets lukewarm (tank is refilling and reheating). Normal behavior for small tank.

## Safety Warnings
**Descaling Hazard": Descaling chemicals can be irritants. Wear gloves if performing descaling.

**Limited Hot Water Planning": Don't reduce supply to critical levels. Must balance hot water needs with electrical safety.

## When To Call A Technician
- Confirmed undersized for household (requires larger unit)
- Heating element weak/failing (replacement needed)
- Insulation significantly degraded (replacement recommended)
- Descaling needed (professional descaling service)

## Estimated Repair Cost (India)
- Descaling service: ₹1500–₹3000
- Heating element replacement: ₹1500–₹3000
- Thermostat adjustment: ₹600–₹1200
- Tank replacement (if upgrading size): ₹5000–₹15000

## Preventive Maintenance
- Descale annually (prevents mineral buildup)
- Check thermostat setting (ensure maximized appropriately)
- Inspect insulation yearly
- Have professional scale removal service annually
- Monitor heating time (gradual increase indicates scale)
