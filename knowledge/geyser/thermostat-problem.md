# Geyser Thermostat Problem

## Overview
Thermostat fails to maintain set temperature. Water either too hot or too cold. Thermostat adjustment doesn't affect output temperature. Caused by faulty thermostat sensor, mechanical jam, or calibration drift.

## Symptoms
- Temperature doesn't reach set value
- Water constantly too hot or too cold
- Turning thermostat dial has no effect
- Temperature fluctuates wildly
- Geyser cycles on/off rapidly
- Cannot adjust temperature up or down
- Thermostat stuck in one position
- No response to temperature adjustments

## Possible Causes
**Faulty Temperature Sensor": Sensor bulb (mercury/electronic) doesn't accurately read temperature.

**Thermostat Calibration Drift": Dial marking no longer matches actual temperature.

**Stuck Thermostat Valve": Valve contacts stuck. Cannot open/close properly.

**Thermal Sensor Contact Broken": Electrical connection to sensor failed.

**Thermostat Bulb Leak": Mercury thermostat bulb cracked. No temperature sensing.

**Mineral Scale on Sensor": Sensor covered with scale. Cannot sense temperature accurately.

## DIY Troubleshooting Steps

1. **Test actual temperature": Use separate thermometer to measure outlet water temperature. Compare to what thermostat shows.

2. **Adjust thermostat dial": Turn slowly from minimum to maximum. Does temperature gradually increase? No change = thermostat stuck or failed.

3. **Wait between adjustments": After adjusting, wait 10 minutes. Geyser needs time to respond. Does temperature eventually change?

4. **Feel thermostat bulb area": If accessible (requires some geyser knowledge), feel the thermostat bulb area (usually inside tank). Very hot even at low setting? Sensor might be stuck.

5. **Check for corrosion": Look at thermostat dial area. Any rust or corrosion around dial mechanism?

6. **Listen to thermostat": When adjusting dial, listen for clicks or mechanical sounds. No sound = mechanical failure.

## Safety Warnings
**Scalding Risk": If thermostat fails stuck to maximum, water can be dangerously hot (>75°C). Risk of severe burns.

**Electrical": Thermostat electrical connections operate at low voltage but handle switching. Do not touch if unsure.

## When To Call A Technician
- Thermostat dial not responding to adjustments
- Temperature cannot be controlled
- Thermostat bulb suspected leaked
- Sensor contact loose or corroded
- Rapid on/off cycling indicating thermostat instability

## Preventive Maintenance
- Test thermostat response monthly
- Monitor for temperature consistency
- Avoid extreme temperature settings (extends thermostat life)
- Descale thermostat area annually (prevents mineral buildup)
- Have annual professional inspection
